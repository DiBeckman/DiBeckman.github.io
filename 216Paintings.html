Art Tour of Paris presentation:
There are two parts to this assignment, one that you begin now and continues through our trip to Paris, and the second when you present one of the four works of art in class.

Part One
The first part of this assignment is to pick one piece of art by January 20 from each of the four museums that we will visit (Cluny, Louvre, D'Orsay, and Pompidou).  Here are two tools you can use to identify your four artworks:

• Our text books and Dr. Marchi's website/web links
• Visit the museum websites (see the links to these sites below)

Once you have identified your artworks, research each piece of art and its artist as well as the historical/artistic significance of each artwork . Record your findings in your Class Portfolio.  Please include your reasoning and rationale for your choices in your findings as well.  We want you to begin to develop a relationship with the four works that you choose.  This relationship will begin now and will culminate with visiting each of your selected pieces in their respective museums while we are in Paris.  We expect that you will have at least two (if not more) entries in your portfolio about your works of art; one recording your initial research and rationale and a second reflecting on your visit to the museum.

Part Two

From the four artworks that you chose for Part One of this assignment, choose one and prepare a two-three minute talk that you will give during our Thursday class meetings. The dates are listed on the Syllabus. We will be strict about this time limit. Your presentation should answer the following questions:

• What is the title of the artwork?
• Who is the artist?
• Where is the artwork located?
• Why did you choose this artwork?

For this last question, you might consider Alain de Botton's 5 answers to the question "What is Art for?"

  1. Art keeps us hopeful.
  2. Art makes us less lonely.
  3. Art rebalances us.
  4. Art helps us to appreciate stuff.
  5. Art is propaganda for what really matters.
Email Dr. Beckman to let her know the artworks you have chosen. Include your first choice for presenting one of the four art works in class.
