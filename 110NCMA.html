The North Carolina Museum of Art- FLF 110 assignment

Visit the Museum's web site for complete information on hours, tours, special exhibits etc. (Note that the NCMA, like most art museums, is closed on Mondays.) You will need to provide your own transportation to the Museum which is located at 2110 Blue Ridge Road, near Rex Hospital unless you come on the class tour and let me know that you need a ride.

As a requirement for FLF 110, you will visit the museum and submit a 2-3 page double-spaced paper about your visit to the Museum. This paper may be in English or French. It should include your impressions of the Museum, including when you went, whether you went alone or with others, and which parts of the museum your visited (inside and out). Focus on at least two works that interest you. This paper may be turned in at any time during the semester but must be turned in by the time you take the Oral Part of the Final Exam during the last week of classes. You may visit the museum on your own or attend one of the daily free tours. I will also organize optional group visits. Dates and times are listed on the Syllabus.

The museum also offers downloadable maps, cell phone tours, and a Sound Track Experience. See http://www.ncartmuseum.org/visit/plan_your_visit/

See this short video introducing the French collection at the NCMA.

In addition, you will present a work of art from the museum's collection at each of your oral exams.

  • For the Midterm Oral Exam, choose a painting where the subject includes people, such as an individual or group portrait, by any artist in the Museum's collection to present to me and your partner. You may choose one of the paintings listed below, as long as it is a portrait and not a landscape.
  • For the Oral Part of the Final Exam, choose any work by a French artist, such as those included below, to present and discuss. You will need to bring copies of these paintings to your exam.

Do some research so that you know the story that is portrayed in the painting and some information the artist (if known). Be prepared to pronounce the artist's name and the work's title correctly in French. See me in class or the office ahead of time for pronunciation practice.

Here's a useful Thinking Routine to get started analyzing your work of art:

LOOKING: TEN TIMES TWO (A routine for observing and describing)

Look at the image quietly for at least 30 seconds. Let your eyes wander.

List 10 words or phrases about any aspect of the picture.

Repeat Steps 1 & 2: Look at the image again and try to list 10 more words or phrases to your list. How do you say those words and phrases in French?
