WELCOME TO FRENCH! This course aims at giving you a solid foundation in understanding, speaking, reading and writing the French language at the elementary level. To accomplish these goals, it is essential that we use French as the language of the classroom. Here are some useful phrases for communicating in class:

Répétez, s'il vous plaît. = Repeat, please.
Comment dit-on [English word]? = How do you say [English word] in French.?
Que veut dire [French word]?= What does [French word] mean ?
Qu'est-ce que c'est? = What's that?
Je ne sais pas. = I don't know.
Je ne comprends pas. = I don't understand.

ATTENDANCE REGULATIONS: Attendance is required and will be taken daily. You can make progress in your language learning only if you hear and speak the language regularly. You are permitted two absences in a class taught Tuesday/ Thursday with no penalty. Each additional absence will reduce your class participation grade by one grade (from A to A-, from B+ to B, etc.). See me as soon as possible after your absence and provide a written excuse to be accepted at my discretion (in case of illness, injury, death or illness in the family, university duties, court attendance, or religious observance.) This policy is based upon the University Attendance Policy. Save your absences for emergencies. Excessive absences may lead to an F for the course.

If you need to miss a class for one of the reasons listed above, let me know before the class meeting, if possible. Upon your return to class after an absence, you are responsible for all work missed and for any assignments announced during your absence. I suggest that you get the name and phone number of a classmate to call in the event that you are unsure of an assignment.

HOMEWORK: Each chapter of the textbook is divided into two parts: 1) Activités et lectures followed by the Vocabulaire section (white pages) and 2) the Grammaire et exercices pages (blue pages).

Before each class, you need to prepare the following:

1) Read and write out the exercises in the appropriate sections of the blue pages. Check your work using a pen or pencil in a different color and the Answer Key in Appendix D of the text book (p. 435). In the margin, jot down any questions you have as you correct your work.

2) Do the written and oral exercises assigned in the Cahier. Exercises with a cassette symbol and exercises with a video-cassette symbol are to be completed with the tapes available in the Foreign Language Technology Center (212 Laundry Bldg.) Correct your workbook exercises using the Answer Key at the end of the Cahier. All exercises should be corrected clearly in a different color. Mark any sections you don't understand so that you can ask about them in class or I can explain when I'm correcting your work.

Due dates for homework (both written and lab exercises) are listed on the syllabus. Full credit will be given only for corrected work turned in on time.

We will cover the two preliminary chapters and chapters 1-5 in FLF 101.

HOW TO DO WELL IN CLASS:
1. Prepare all your assignments before coming to class so that we can devote our in-class sessions to communicative activities rather than the learning of simple grammar rules. The auditory listening program in the Language Lab is also particularly important in this regard. Assignments are due for class on the day indicated on the syllabus. Don't get behind or allow yourself to remain confused.

2. ACTIVELY participate in each lesson. Mentally answer each and every question by practicing each answer under your breath while a classmate is responding.

3. TAKE RISKS!! Speak out. Don't be afraid to make mistakes. The more you speak, the better you will get.

4. Speak French in the classroom. Speak French outside the classroom at every opportunity.

5. Do not chew gum in class. Having something in your mouth makes it harder to use correct pronunciation.

5. Enjoy your language learning. Be playful and participate in games, skits, etc.

6. TAKE NOTES. Write down everything from the blackboard. If you don't have enough time in class to get everything down, I will be happy to share materials with you after class or in my office.

I look forward to a pleasant and productive semester! Don't hesitate to ask me for help and assistance in person or by email.
