Dates, days, months and holidays

The days of the week

Note that French uses lower-case letters for the names of the days.

Write the names of the days in full, do not abbreviate as in English.

The French week begins on Monday, unlike American calendars which start on Sunday

Monday=lundi
Tuesday- mardi
Wednesday= mercredi
Thursday= jeudi
Friday= vendredi
Saturday= samedi
Sunday= dimanche

lundi below stands in for any day; they all work the same way

**Note the use of le for regular occurences, and no article for single ones. Do not translate "on" in English as sur in French in the expression "on Mondays", on the weekend, etc: I work on Mondays-> Je travaille le lundi. I went to the movies on Monday-> Lundi, je suis allé au cinéma. This error will cost you 1/2 point on tests.**

on Monday lundi
on Mondays, every Monday le lundi
What day is it? Quel jour sommes-nous?
Monday afternoon lundi après-midi
last Monday night la nuit de lundi dernier
early on Monday lundi matin de bonne heure
late on Monday lundi soir tard
last/ next Monday lundi dernier/ prochain
from Monday on à partir de lundi
   

The months of the year

As with days of the week, do not use capitals to spell the names of the months in French, and do not abbreviate as in English.

January= janvier
February= février
March= mars
April= avril
May= mai
June= juin
July= juillet
August= août
September= septembre
October= octobre
November= novembre
Deember= décembre

May in the following example stands for any month; they all work the same way

in May en mai, au mois de mai
next May en mai prochain
last May l'année dernière en mai
in early/ late May début/ fin mai

Dates
French has only one generally accepted way of writing dates: le 10 mai. Note that the date comes before the month in French, although in English we put the month and then the day. If the day of the week is included, put it after the le. Note the absence of commas in French dates compared to English dates.

Monday, May 1st, 1901 le lundi 1er mai 1901
What's the date? Quel jour sommes-nous?
It's the 10th of May. Nous sommes/ on est le dix mai
in 1968 en 1968
in the year 2000 en l'an deux mille
in the seventeenth century au dix-septième siècle
