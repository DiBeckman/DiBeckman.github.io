Stories based on Pirandello's 6 Characters in Search of an Author

Act Four

The audience applauds to an empty stage, assuming that what they had seen was planned to begin and end as such.  Accordingly, they all file out of the auditorium with a sense of appreciation for the contrast of reality and fiction they have just witnessed.

            With the curtain sill down, and the PRODUCER, ACTORS, FATHER, MOTHER, and SON still behind it, the STEPDAUGHTER suddenly reappears among them.  The PRODUCER and the ACTORS are still frozen in disbelief, but no more confused than the STEPDAUGHTER herself.

            The STEPDAUGHTER, after running out into the foyer and beyond, magically ends back up on stage with her parents, brother, and others, as if the stage stood outside the auditorium doors.  The auditorium is now empty, but the curtain yet again rises with all the characters present except for the LITTLE BOY and the LITTLE GIRL. 

PRODUCER    [Looking around at everybody in disbelief.]   Look!  It’s empty!  They’re all gone!  We’ve done it, we’ve done it!  Don’t you see, everyone that came to see our rehearsal thought that what happened here today was in fact the rehearsal itself.  Brilliant!

STEPDAUGHTER    [Still confused as ever.]   What am I doing here?  It’s finished!  I am free! I am free right?  Mother, why can I not leave this hideous place already?  All this time I thought that once we finished our story, we would all be free.  And now it’s finished, so I should be free from that horrible creature! [She points to the FATHER.]

FATHER    No.  We are never free from the story to which we belong.  We were created in one story only, and we will not ever have lives outside of that story.  So you can run as fast as you can, but I’m afraid that you will never be able to escape us, the characters created in that same story as your own.

                        [The STEPDAUGHTER begins to sob as she listens to her father’s explanation of her                                     reappearance.  The MOTHER and SON also seem terrified as though this conclusion                                     was conflicting with their own ideas of what their futures held.]

            I, too, was unsure of what was to happen after the story was completed, but now, I see it all clearly.  We will remain together forever, never growing older, doing anything different, or experiencing another emotion, nothing other than how our original author wanted us to exist. 

                        [The PRODUCER, after intently listening to the FATHER’S theory of these characters’                                     lives from this point onward, sneakily runs off stage to his office.  The ACTORS remain                                     in a state of confusion as to whether any of the day’s events were a display of reality or                                     just a mere act of fiction; they continue to intently listen while trying to put together the                                     array of the day’s puzzle pieces.]

SON    But Father, I cannot live with this woman any longer. [Pointing to his Mother.]  It is obvious that she cannot live with us for long either.  You must do something, Father, you must do something now!

MOTHER    [Still sobbing, and with a baffled sound in her voice.]  All I wanted is the best for everyone.  Where are the rest of my children?  [To the STEPDAUGHTER]  Bring them back. You came back, so where are they?  Did you see them out in the foyer?  Where are my youngest children!?

SON    Of course my existence wouldn’t be good enough for the likes of someone such as you.  I’ve never been enough, and I’m certainly not now.

STEPDAUGHTER    [Angrily to the SON.]  Do you have to act like a baby at a time like this? Mother, don’t you get it yet?  That despicable imposter of a father is actually right.  We are all just characters.  And, of course I didn’t see my siblings in the foyer, they are dead,  Mother.  That was their role in the story, to die.  And now that they have done so and we have finally acted the scene out all the way through, they are gone, gone for good.

FATHER   [As if with a new idea in his voice.]  Wait a minute!  If they are gone for good and not coming back, then that means that we are still at the end of our story.  But when does our story begin again?  When will the children reappear?  Why have they not done so yet?  What if…

STEPDAUGHTER    [Rudely interrupting.]  Don’t be so silly old man.  I am not acting out that scene with you again!  There will not be another beginning to our story.  We did what we have come to do, and now we are finished!

FATHER    Actually, you are wrong!  We have not done what we have come to do.  All we have done since we have been here is act out what has already been written for us.  The story in which we have presented is just the unfinished story in which we have lived in since our creation.  But we did not come just to act out what has already been written for us!  We came in search of an author to finish our story!

[Just at this time, the PRODUCER runs in waiving some paper around as if it were aflame.  The ACTORS, startled at the surprise entrance, thus far have been quiet in listening to the characters ponder their existence.  They have now completely turned into  the audience to the characters’ play.]

PRODUCER    I’ve got it!  It’s finished!  I’ve finished your story!  [Running to all four of the characters, he hands out his papers.]  Here, read over it quickly, so we can start rehearsing the end.  Come on now, quickly!  Don’t just stand there like a bunch of masked statues, read it!

FATHER    [To the PRODUCER.]  You’ve already finished our story?

STEPDAUGHTER    [With an angry tone towards the PRODUCER.]  You didn’t even ask us how we wanted our own story to end!?

MOTHER    [To the PRODUCER in a solemn tone.]  Is it a sad ending?

PRODUCER    [At this point, frustrated by the lack of confidence they’ve all expressed in his abilities.]  Will you all just sit down and read it!

  [The characters read the script the PRODUCER has created for them and talk quietly                        among themselves.  After doing so, somehow they all seem to agree on the ending the                         PRODUCER has given to them.  They start acting out the ending with the PRODUCER                        yelling out “cut” and “perfect,” fore he believes that the story of the six characters, now                             more of his own, will present itself on opening night in the place of his originally planned                         play.

 The ACTORS indifferently watch the ending to this story, caring less that they have                         become an audience to other characters.  Throughout the confusingly, long day, they have been drawn into the story of the six characters, but more so, into its present fixation   on the realistic effects that this fictional story includes.

 The FATHER makes peace with his stepdaughter.  He regains the love of his   former wife, and the control of the family.  The MOTHER gains the love of her son and    forms a strong, loving connection with her former husband.  The STEPDAUGHTER completely forgives her father and rids her soul of all anger, leaving only room for love. The SON forgives his mother and embraces a long, lost family.

As the characters say their last lines in the script, each slowly begins to visually fade,             blending in with the background.  Soon, they all are becoming invisible, and with the last             word of the script read aloud, they are gone.  At that instant, all four scripts sway                         downward to the floor, ever so smoothly and softly.  Amazement consumes the actors,             and an overwhelming freedom overtakes the story of the six characters in search of an             author.]
