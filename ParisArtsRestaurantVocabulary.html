Restaurant Vocabulary: adapted from http://french.about.com

Types of menus
• Le menu and la formule refer to the fixed-price menu, which includes two or more courses (with limited choices for each) and is usually the cheapest (and in some restaurants, the only) way to eat.
 
• The menu that the waiter hands you is la carte - anything you order from it is à la carte
 
• La carte des vins is the wine menu
 
• Une dégustation is a tasting menu, with small servings of multiple dishes (déguster means "to taste")

Courses
A French meal may include numerous courses, in this strict order:
1. Apéritif - cocktail, pre-dinner drink
 
2. Amuse-bouche or amuse-gueule - snack (just one or two bites)
 
3. Entrée - appetizer/starter (false cognate alert: entree means "main course" in English)
 
4. Plat principal - main course
 
5. Salade verte- green salad

6. Fromage - cheese
 
7. Dessert - dessert
 
8. Café - coffee
 
9. Digestif - after-dinner drink
 
Special terms
• Le plat du jour is the daily special
 
• Ardoise means "slate"; it refers to the specials board
 
• L’addition is the check or bill. If you need a receipt, ask for un reçu. Normally, the waiter will not bring the bill until you ask for it.

• Gratuit and offert both mean free; prix nets and service compris means that the tip is included in the bill, as is typical in France. Une carafe d'eau fraîche is the free pitcher of water that waiters are required by law to bring you IF you ask for it. If you just ask for water (de l'eau), you will get bottled water (de l'eau minérale). If you order bottled water, you will be asked if you want gazeuse (sparkling) or plate (without bubbles).

• The waiter will often add the word petit to his offer: Un petit dessert ? Un petit café ?
 
• The waiter will often ask “Ça a éte?” when coming to collect your plate. It means, “Did you enjoy it?” “Oui, merci.” would be polite responses. "Merci", all by itself in response to an offer of food, means "no".

•When you're full, do not say "Je suis pleine" (which refers instead to pregnancy) say “Non, merci. J'ai bien mangé."
