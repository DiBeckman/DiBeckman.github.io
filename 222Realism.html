Counterpoint to romanticism

Focus on “truth” rather than the “fantastic, historical, remote, idealized, unsullied, idyllic” (839) “what is” (the real) vs. “what should be” (the ideal)

Honesty. Realism: Philippe Duranty: “the exact, complete and sincere reproduction of the social milieu in which we live”; Henri Bayle: “A novel is a mirror walking along the road.”

The novel rather than poetry. Why?
1) Varied aspects of society
2) New readership; easily accessible newspapers and journals

Novelist as sociologist. Observation and objectivity. Portray and criticize society.

Subject matter: commonplace, even the low, the disgusting. Serious purpose, often tragic.

Objectivity in narration: Author shows rather than tells; should be like God in his universe “present everywhere but visible nowhere.” (Flaubert)
