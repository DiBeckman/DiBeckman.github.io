Class 16 (Flaubert 5) due Friday, Sept. 29
Finish your character sketch and turn in. Choose any one of the following questions about Madame Bovary to answer. Include the question, please, with your answer. These questions would also inspire some good discussions in your Web CT posts.

Coming up: peer review draft of Paper 1 is due next Wednesday, Oct. 4. Read about it. Think about it.

FORMALIST CRITICISM:
• How do various elements such as characterization create and reinforce the work's underlying meaning? For example, is Abbé Bournisien's role, not to disrespect, but to call attention to the failure of organized religion to meet people's needs?
• Why did Flaubert pick Madame Bovary as a title?

SOCIOLOGICAL CRITICISM
  Marxist Criticism:
• What character do you think portrays the failure of the bourgeoisie the most? Which characters contribute to it?
• Money plays an extremely important role in the characters' lives in Madame Bovary. How do economic conditions relate to the characters' attitudes and conditions in the story?
• How did Madame Bovary's social and economic status affect her abilty to borrow money?
• What is Flaubert trying to tell the reader about the current social structure in France. Does he agree with having class distinctions or is he suggesting that there needs to be reform?
• Explain the differences and similarities between Charles, Charles' father, Charles' mother and Emma in regard to their perspective on economic and social forces that affect their lives.

SOCIOLOGICAL CRITICISM
  Feminist Criticism:
• How do the relationships between men and women in Madame Bovary compare to those of the Enlightenment and Romanticism, especially in considering the theme of power?
• How does Emma's status in society as a woman drive her actions?
• How did Flaubert use Emma to show his feelings about a woman's role in society? Emma is seen as provocative, childish, and only good at seduction. Are these Flaubert's feelings or society's feelings at the time?
• Emma always seems to think that "the grass is always green on the other side of the fence." Does her attitude cause the conflicts she has with Charles? Does Charles have any clue about Emma and her love for Léon and now for Rodolphe? Will Emma's eloping with Rodolphe really solve the problemss she has with being happy in love, or is she just searching for something she will never find?
• What are some examples of the gender roles in the story, and how do they affect the relationships between the characters?
• What about Emma is different from the typical lady of her rank and time period, and what statement, if any, is Flaubert making through her character?
• How does the feminist movement stand in Emma's walks of life? Does Emma reject these roles and ideals?
• How are Emma's and the elder Madame Bovary's lives portrayed in Madame Bovary? Does Emma accept or reject her role as Charles' wife? How?
• How is Emma's marriage to Charles representative of realism and how does her relationship with him compare to her relationships with the other men in her life: her father, Léon, and Rodophe?
• Why does Emma feel so repressed by her gender instead of empowered by it?

CULTURAL AND HISTORICAL CRITICSM:
• What elements of Madame Bovary make this a realist work? What parallels are there between this story and the cultural and historical aspect of its era?
• Even though Charles is not the best at his profession, he is a doctor. Why is this seen as a lowly profession during this time period, even those it is a respectable one in modern times?
• As Emma begins her fantasies of true love and romance at an early age in the convent, has she set herself up to be unsatisfied by constantly dreaming up "fairy tale" love affairs? After being married and carrying on two "love" affairs, she remains discontent, leaving the reader to wonder if she will ever stop her flightiness accept the path her life has followed.
• Emma wanted so badly to move up into the life of the upper class with all the fine dresses, balls and plays. Were there cultural and/or social barriers to her living a life that she would have been more happy with or was Charles the main barrier?
• What does Flaubert's attack on romanticism in Madame Bovary say about his personal views of life and the society in which he lives?
• How does Emma's character compare with the ideals of the Cult of Domesticity and the ideals of womanhood in the 19th century?
• What societal issues were raised when people read about Emma Bovary and her relationships with several men? How did society's views and expectations begin to change at this time?
• How does Madame Bovary reflect the time period (mid-1800's) in which it was written?
• How does Emma's education or lack thereof ultimately lead to her destruction?
• How much blame do you impute to the characters, and how much do you lay at the feet of that culture's social norms and mores?
• Was Emma the symbol of the struggle of the Bourgeoisie to gain wealth and to escape the lonesome middle class? Was Emma used by Flaubert to make fun or criticize the Bourgeoisie's ignorance and failure to live a life beneficial to society?
• How does the time period in which the story is based affect the characters' lives differently than if they lived today

PSYCHOLOGICAL CRITICISM:
• What do Emma's emotions and behaviors reveal about her psychological state? What is her personality type?
• Does Emma have actual psychological problems that have provoked her behavior, such as lust, shopping sprees, hair colors/cuts? Is Flaubert hinting at his own psychological state through the flaws and problems of the various characters, such as lust, lying, failure?
• Does Flaubert present desires of the characterss consciously or unconsciously?
• How does the work reflect Flaubert's personal feelings on the situation in society and what symbolism does he use to show this situation?
• What are some of the psychological issues, in primary characters, that are obvious to the reader but not to the other characters?
• Because Flaubert is writing about unstable, overbearing and frail women as the center of his story, what does this say about Flaubert's relationships with women in the past and the present? What events in his life could have caused Flaubert to have low respect for women in his life? What might lead the reader to think that this disrespect might have stemmed from someone in Flaubert's life?

READER RESPONSE CRITICISM:
• What was your reaction to Emma's decision to have an affair with Léon in Part 3? Why?
• Which character do you most relate to? How did this affect the way you view that character and/or his/her role in the story?
• With which character do the readers sympathize with the most?
• As a reader, how do you respond to this story? Also how you do respond to the life-line and life which Emma is living?
• Is Emma unrealistic in her desire for personal fulfillment and happiness? How do your expectations of personal fulfillment affect your reading of Madame Bovary? How do you judge the character's actions and decisions based on your own moral/ ethical views?

Some helpful background to understand the family drama which precedes Act 1:
Father, an intellectual, marries a lower class woman; they have a son, but the Father becomes bored with his wifeand sends her off to live with his male secretary. Father keeps custody of the Son, who is raised by a wet nurse and grows up to be very resentful. The wife obediently leaves the family home and she and the former secretary have 3 more children; the Father watches over them, but eventually they move away; the stepfather dies, and the wife and three children move back to the original town without telling the Father. Mother and Stepdaughter take jobs with a dressmaker, Madame Pace, who runs a call-girl service: Mother as a seamstress, Stepdaughter as a prostitute, who is ultimately hired by the Father. Family is reunited: explosive conflict.
