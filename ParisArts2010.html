“Known by many names—‘The City of Lights,’ ‘The City of Love,’ ‘Gay Paree”—Paris is . . . one of Europe’s oldest and most celebrated cities. Behind the famous monuments such as the Eiffel Tower, the Louvre museum, and the Champs-Elysées, Paris is a bustling cosmopolitan metropolis, with business of all kinds and colorful local culture, enriched by decades of immigration from France’s former colonies. The city prides itself on the beauty of its architecture and has strict laws about building inside the city limits in order to preserve its charm. It is also associated with the world’s best gastronomy, fashion, and hospitality. Paris has its origins in Roman times, as evidenced by ancient ruins within the city.” To understand European and American history, understanding Paris is essential. This class will introduce students to the French language (a major influence on English), and look at the role Paris played and still plays on the world stage.

There are no pre-requisites for the course. The language of instruction is English. There will be intensive French tourist language instruction as part of on-campus, pre-departure classes. Students proficient in French will be able to submit the final paper in French.

Course Objectives: (GEP Visual and Performing Arts)
The course objectives are for the student to:
• Engage the human experience through the interpretation of human culture and
• Become aware of the act of interpretation itself as a critical form of knowing in the humanities; and
• Observe and reflect on the history and art of Paris, France through their class participation, research and writing, using evidence and arguments appropriate to the humanities.

•• March 12-21: Spring Break in Paris (departure March 12, return March 21).
Each day in Paris will have minimum two hours guided walking, bike or boat tour and/or museum visit. We will also see two Ionesco plays.

• Day 2, Saturday 13/03
Class: Welcome to Paris. Orientation to the 14th Arrondissement including visit to the Catacombs. 1 hour Seine cruise entrance ticket with Bateaux Parisiens. Dinner at the Monte Carlo.

• Day 3, Sunday 14/03
Class: Paris History. Fat Tire Bike Tour: Eiffel Tower, Ecole Militaire, Napoleon’s Tomb, Musée Rodin, Les, Invalides, Pont Alexandre, Grand and Petit Palais, Place de la Concorde, Champs-Elysées, Arc de Triomphe. Tuileries Garden, Louvre Museum. Visit to the Musée Carnavalet, the museum of the history of Paris. Visit the Marais and Ile de la Cité, including Notre Dame de Paris.

• Day 4, Monday 15/03
Class: Roman and Medieval Paris. 10 am. Walking Tour: The Medieval Latin Quarter (Métro Maubert Mutualité).
Afternoon: Visit of the Musée Nation du Moyen Age - the present collection provides a valuable insight into the richness of artistic activity in the Middle Ages and a valuable witness of medieval society in Europe - and the Arènes de Lutèce. Visit to St Chapelle- represents one of the most complete examples of the stained-glass window art of that time - & the Cathedral of Notre-Dame

• Day 5, Tuesday 16/03
Class: Liberté, Egalité, Fraternité The Enlightenment & Revolutionary Paris. 2 pm Walking Tour: Revolution (Métro Odéon at the statue). Includes visit to place de la Concorde where the guillotine stood, as well as the Palais Royal, where new ideas were debated, the site of the radical Jacobin club and the ill-fated Tuileries palace. Hear of Marie Antoinette, Equality Philip, Robespierre, and Charlotte Corday. See the historic Latin Quarter where the revolutionaries lived and met, the oldest café in Paris, and the hall where Danton and the radical Cordeliers' club held their debates.

• Day 6, Wednesday 17/03
Class: Renaissance and Classical France; The Empire. The Palace and Art Museum of the Louvre. Napoléon and the Arc de Triomphe.
Walking Tour: Taste of Paris: 3-5.30pm (Métro Etienne Marcel)
Evening: théâtre de la Huchette: Ionesco’s La Leçon et La Cantatrice Chauve.

• Day 7, Thursday 18/03
Class: Ancien Régime and Absolutism. Half-Day tour to Versailles, by RER line C public transport with English-speaking guide. Versailles: Architecture and Gardens.

• Day 8, Friday 19/03
Class: Impressionism and beyond. Art in Paris in the 19th and early 20th century. Museum visit at lunch at Musée d’Orsay. Walking Tour: Village Montmartre 10 am (Métro Abbesses) : Visit to Montmartre winding streets, the vineyard, artists' studios (Renoir, Lautrec, Van Gogh) quiet gardens, historic cabarets, the place-du-Tertre with its artists and the Sacré Coeur Basilica.

• Day 9, Saturday 20/03
Class: Paris from La Belle époque to Modernity. Independent visits: Musée Rodin, Quai Branly, Centre Pompidou, Musée de la mode.
