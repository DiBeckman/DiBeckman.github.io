Sample answer to the literary movement question on the Midterm-thanks, Sonia.

Realism is characterized by its lack of flowery language, little
enamoration with nature, and its prediliction for the scientific method. A
realist novel is like a mirror going down the street (class notes.) The
author's job is to describe what is happening and sometimes to arrange
events in a logical sequence.

Gustave Flaubert spared no details when he discussed the gory details of
Hippolyte's clubfoot operationg in his novel, Madame Bovary. He was
vivid in his description of the gangrene, and Hippolyte's doomed
"convalescense." The beggar on the highway was also described in graphic
detail-- his skin problems, missing eyes, and possible scrofula were all
presented in the text. The character Homais was presented for what he was,
with judicious comments from the narrator, showing his shallow,
ambition-grubbing nature.

Leo Tolstoy's Death of Ivan Ilyich is another realistic story. No
concessions are made to polite society; people are shown exactly as they
are. When Ivan's dead, his "friends'" motivations are uncovered by teh
narrator from the start. The smell of Ivan's corpse is not overlooked and
all of the sensory data in the story are made available to the reader.

Realism is harsher compared to Romanticism; the Romantics valued Nature's
beauty and mystery. Enlightenment writers valued education and the
scientific method. It was geared more towards man's greatness than man's
reality.
