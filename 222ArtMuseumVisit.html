The North Carolina Museum of Art — ENG/FL 222 assignment

Visit the Museum's web site for complete information on hours, tours, special exhibits etc. (Note that the NCMA, like most art museums, is closed on Mondays.) You will need to provide your own transportation to the Museum which is located at 2110 Blue Ridge Road, near Rex Hospital.

As a requirement for ENG/FL 222, you will visit the museum and submit a 2-3 page paper about your visit to the Museum. It should include your impressions of the Museum and focus on at least two works that interest you. You could also discuss which parts of the museum you visited, who went with you, how seeing art works affects how you think about the literature we have been studying, etc. This paper, which has the same weight as 3 homework assignments, may be turned in anytime during the semester, but must be turned in by April 4. As part of midterm and the final exams, you will be asked to include two non-literary works of art (painting, sculpture or music) to illustrate the different literary periods covered in the short essay (Part II) section of the exam. It makes sense to select these works ahead of time. I recommend that you choose at least one from the North Carolina Museum.

You may visit the museum on your own or at one of the daily guided tours. I will also organize two optional group tours for our class, one for the Enlightenment-Realist periods (Friday, February 19, from 7-8:30 pm) and one for Modernism (Friday, April 1, 7-8:30 pm). You will receive extra credit for attending the group tour. Email me to sign up.

The museum also offers downloadable maps, cell phone tours, and a Sound Track (MP3) Experience. See http://www.ncartmuseum.org/visit/plan_your_visit/

Here's a useful Thinking Routine to get started analyzing your work of art:

LOOKING: TEN TIMES TWO (A routine for observing and describing)

Look at the image quietly for at least 30 seconds. Let your eyes wander.

List 10 words or phrases about any aspect of the picture.

Repeat Steps 1 & 2: Look at the image again and try to list 10 more words or phrases to your list.
