ACTIVITÉ: Vous allez dans un pays francophone
Sign up: Friday, October 22
Draft of handout due: Wednesday, October 27
Présentation orale: Monday and Wednesday, November 1 and 3

For this project, you will use the Internet to research a French-speaking country, complete a written assignment, prepare a handout for your classmates, and orally present in French the material to the class on the due date. Please see the Assessment Guide for Oral Presentations for the class presentation. The writing assignment will be graded according to the Writing Assessment Rubric. You will also be responsible for taking notes during the presentations as preparation for Exam 4.

1. Find a partner.

2. Choose one of the French-speaking countries (les pays francophones) from the list below. Sign up with me. First come, first served. See the maps at the beginning of our textbook to locate your country.

3. Decide who will do questions A and who will do questions B. Find the answers to the questions indicated. You may use the Internet. I suggest you go to http://fr.yahoo.com/ or http://www.google.fr/ and type in the name of your country.

Using French web sites will actually make things easier since you won't make translation mistakes. If you can't find the answer to a particular question, find another interesting or relevant fact. See Module 4 to review leisure activities, Module 5 to review weather, and Module 9 for travel vocabulary.

4. Prepare a one-page handout for your classmates with highlights or interesting facts about your country. It should be at least 50% in French. Turn in a draft of the handout the Wednesday before the presentation (October 27), so that I can proofread the French.

5. On a separate sheet, write a two paragraphs to tell about the your country (Part I) and the trip you took (Part II). Skip lines or double space. Use present tense verbs to describe the country and past tense verbs (passé composé and imparfait) to say what you did there. Coordinate with your partner.

6. On the due date, you and your partner will orally present your work to the class. Be prepared to point out your country on the world map provided. Come in costume, bring a food, print out pictures or make a poster to illustrate your presentation. Speak directly to the class using notes and your handout and visual aids, rather than reading your report! Turn in your written assignment after your presentation.
