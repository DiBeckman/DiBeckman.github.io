Dates, days and Months

The days of the week

Note that French uses lower-case letters for the anmes of the days.

Write the names of the days in full, do not abbreviate as in English.

The French week begins on Monday, unlike American calendars which start on Sunday

Monday=lundi
Tuesday- mardi
Wednesday= mercredi
Thursday= jeudi
Friday= vendredi
Saturday= samedi
Sunday= dimanche

lundi below stands in for any day; they all work the same way

Note the use of le for regular occurences, and no article for single ones. Do not translate on
on Mondays

on Monday lundi
on Mondays le lundi
What day is it? Quel jour sommes-nous?
Monday afternoon lundi après-midi
last Monday night la nuit de lundi dernier
early on Monday lundi matin de bonne heure
late on Monday lundi soir tard
last/ next Monday lundi dernier/ prochain
from Monday iob à partir de lundi
   

The months of the year

As with days of the week, do not use capitals to spell the names of the months in French, and do not abbreviate as in English.

January= janvier
February= février
March= mars
April= avril
May= mai
June= juin
July= juillet
August= août
September= septembre
October= octobre
November= novembre
Deember= décembre

May in the following example stands for any month; they all work the same way

in May en mai, au mois de mai
next May en mai prochain
last May l'année dernière en mai
in early/ late May début/ fin mai

Dates

French has only one generally accepted way of writing dates: le 10 mai. If the day of the week is included, put it after the le
