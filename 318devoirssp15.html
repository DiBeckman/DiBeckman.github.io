Des informations pertinentes des Course Information Pages:

LEARNING GOALS

By the end of the course you should be able to:

  • explain the characteristics of the major periods of French cinema from its invention to the present day comparing "ideas, values, images, cultural artifacts, economic structures, technological developments, [and] attitudes." (General Education Program, NCSU)
  • describe how a film reflects its historical context.
  • identify the narrative and cinematic devices employed in a film, such as theme, point of view, mise-en scène, and composition, and relate them to the content of the film. See Glossaries in Apprentissage (3-7) and A Short Guide (180-183) for definitions of these terms.
  • discuss, orally and in writing, selected French films and their continuing aesthetic validity or challenge.
  • write a coherent essay on given topics about these films and their contexts, supporting arguments with evidence. The learning objective is for students to "make academic arguments about the human experience using reasons and evidence for supporting those reasons that are appropriate to the humanities." (General Education Program, NCSU).

EXAMS

There will be a midterm (15% of final grade) and a final exam (15% of final grade) as indicated on the Syllabus. Make-up for exams will be granted only if you are excused in advance with proper documentation.

Vous pouvez apporter un dictionnaire, votre glossaire et une fiche cartonnée 3’’ X 5’’ (note card) préparée à l’avance. Apportez aussi un/deux « bluebooks » ou du papier. Si vous arrivez sans papier ou sans stylo/crayon, vous aurez une réduction automatique de 5 points sur la note finale de l'examen.

Midterm: As part of the midterm (20 points out of 100) you will prepare and turn in your own Glossaire du cinéma. It should include a minimum of 40 terms in French and English and an illustrative example (in words or with an image) from a film you have seen for 20 of those terms. Here is a template to give you an idea.

For both exams, I will show you clips that we have discussed in class as well a short film that we have not and ask you to write about them, using appropriate vocabulary and connections with films seen during the semester. Use the first three sets of questions on the Question Page as a prompt for your studying and writing. The Scoring Rubric (for the term project) shows how your papers will be graded and what to strive for in the areas of content, organization and format, grammar, vocabulary and fluency. I will be looking for the same qualities on the exams, although my expectations are different since the exams are timed.

Timely warning from Corrigan, page 66: "Do not simply describe technical details and expect them to be self-explanatory. Rather, put them to work to convey an idea about the various ways that frames and their points of view operate and what they mean in specific films, in specific cultures, and at specific times."

1. Avant de visionner le film. Répondez à ces questions pour vérfiier vos connaissances de la fin de la Seconde Guerre mondiale. Citez vos ressources. (NB: My sincere apologies if I corrected your use of Seconde vs. Deuxième guerre mondiale in your journals.     • Que veut dire le mot japonais hibakusha?
