March 5 — MARCH 13: SPRING BREAK IN PARIS (updated Feb. 23)

  • Day 1, Saturday, Departure from North Carolina, USA.

  • Day 2, Sunday. Arrival in Paris. Visit neighborhood. 1 hour Seine cruise entrance ticket with Bateaux Parisiens. Nap and unpack when the rooms are ready. Visit of the 15eme Arrondissement with Marie, a local and NCSU MA grad. Visit of the Eiffel Tower at 10 pm.

  • Day 3, Monday. Morning: Fat Tire Bike Tour: Eiffel Tower, Ecole Militaire, Napoleon's Tomb, Musée Rodin, Les Invalides, Pont Alexandre, Grand and Petit Palais, Place de la Concorde, Arc de Triomphe, Tuileries Garden; Lunch in the Tuileries Garden. Louvre Museum. Free afternoon. Early group dinner followed by visit to the Pompiodou Center.

  • Day 4, Tuesday. 9 am: Visit the inside of Notre Dame. 10 am Walking tour: The Islands and the Marais. An ideal walk for those who want one tour to capture the essence of Paris in two hours on food. We start on Île de la Cité, to trace the early development of Paris, and then look at the superb sculptures and architecture of Notre Dame Cathedral. The tour includes the poignant memorial to the Deported, the romantic Île St Louis with its tree-lined quaysides, and then we explore the historic Maris district with its beautiful architecture and finish at the oldest square in Paris, the lovely Place des Vosges. Afternoon: 3 pm. Meet in front of the Opéra Garnier for 15h30 guided tour. Early evening: Walking tour of the République area with Christine, another Parisian friend and group dinner.

  • Day 5, Wednesday. AM: Visit fo the Cluny Museum. Lunch on your own in the Latin Quarter. Afternoon: 3pm. Montmartre Walking Tour; Meet at métro Abbesses (Meet at metro Abbesses (use the elevator to get to street level, the station is deep!) On this picturesque walk you will discover old winding streets, the vineyard, attractive artists' studios (Renoir, Lautrec, Van Gogh) quiet gardens, historic cabarets, the famous Lapin Agile cabaret, the place-du-Tertre with its street artists and portrait sketchers, the Sacré Coeur Basilica and a stunning view over the city. Free time after the walking tour. The Louvre is open until 9:45 pm.

  • Day 6, Thursday. Morning: Half-Day tour to Versailles, by RER line C public transport with English-speaking guide. Versailles: Architecture and Gardens. Free time after the Guided Visit, and free evening.

  • Day 7, Friday. Morning: Group Visit to Orsay Museum. 12 Noon: meet at Restaurant on the Second Floor for Group meal. Afternoon: 3 pm: Fashion in the Palais Royal Neighborhood walking tour. Explore this attractive district with the lovely covered arcades which is now home to modern fashion designers. We begin with the place where fashion boutiques first started in the early 19th century, in the charming Palais Royal area, where we now see show selling upscale designer clothes (and one where stars do their shopping). We see the boutiques of Stella McCartney, the fabulous shoes of Louboutin and some unique small scale boutiques for fashion, perfume and design. Our fashion specialist will talk about the history of fashion, as well as designers of today. Meet at Métro Palais-Royal-Musée du Louvre, at the exit on place Colette, besides the Comédie-Française. The Métro exit is a distinctive modern one of silver and colored beads. Free evening.

  • Day 8, Saturday: Free day. Great day to visit the Louvre and finish your Top 10 list. Farewell Group dinner. Time and place to be determined.

  • Day 9, Sunday: Departure and Return to the US.
