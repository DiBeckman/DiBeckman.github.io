Presentations; Mémoire

Presentations: The class presentations will be on the last four days of class. Each presentation should be approximately 10 minutes long, including a clip of not more than 5 minutes to share with the class. You will also post a fiche pedagogique on the Google Docs page the day of your presentation. Presentations will be arranged chronologically. The presentation will be graded by the entire class based on this rubric.

La Structure de la Présentation

  • De quel genre de film s'agit-il?
  • Résumez l'intrigue du film. Expliquez la structure et les thèmes du film et notez le vocabulaire essentiel ou spécifique au film.
  • Décrivez les personnages principaux.
  • Choisissez une scène et commentez cette scène et sa technique cinématographique.
  • Comment est-ce que ce film fait partie de l'héritage du cinéma français?
  • Qu'est-ce que vous avez apprécié le plus dans le film?

Mémoire (Paper): You will write a paper in French (approximately 6-9 pages including illustrations and works cited), based on the notes in your journal and your presentation. For an idea of models to follow, see the chapters on each film in Singerman (without the dossier critique), as well as Corrigan's description of a "Critical Essay" (13-15; with sample essays 81-5; 100-111; 154-160). The complete rough draft is due on the day of your presentation, April 13-22. This draft may be turned in as hard copy or shared on Google Docs.

Review the checklist from Corrigan and the Scoring Rubric below to see what qualities I expect in your paper and what will determine your grade. A Short Guide to Writing About Film Chapters 5-7 includes details about what is expected for style, research and manuscipt form. See also: Vocabulaire utile pour exprimer une opinion; pour écrire en français; pour nos films; Correction symbols and abbreviations. Site génial pour la révision de la grammaire.

Checklist:

1. Do I understand the film or films I intend to discuss?
2. Are my notes clear and complete, allowing me to describe and respond to key images, scenes, and other elements in the film?
3. Does my opening paragraph lead to a specific and precisely formulated thesis that anticipates the main point of the essay?
4. Do my topic sentences reflect a logical development of that thesis?
5. Are there smooth transitions between paragraphs and sentences?
6. Do paragraphs cohere, usually around a single idea?
7. Is the meaning of each sentence clear, and are the structures of sentences varied?
8. Are general or abstract observations supported with concrete examples?
9. Have I carefully proofread and revised for grammatical, spelling and typographical errors?
10. Have footnotes and quotations been double-checked for accuracy and proper placement?

Scoring Rubric

CONTENT Excellent to very good: knowledgeable; substantive, thorough development of the thesis, including appropriate examples; quotations and illustrations are well chosen to support the argument; quotations and illustrations are well integrated and presented correctly, good analysis and synthesis of the material; good use of comparison and contrast, critical inquiry and interpretation, relevant to the topic chosen.
ORGANIZATION AND FORMAT Excellent to very good: clear statement of ideas; title that orients the reader to the thesis; clear organization (beginning, middle, and end) and smooth transitions; introduction leads reader into topic; logical and cohesive sequencing both between and within paragraphs, conclusion effectively summarizes main findings and follows logically from the analysis presented; Any quotations/footnotes/images properly cited; length and legibility appropriate.
GRAMMAR AND FLUENCY Excellent to very good: fluent expression; accurate use of relatively complex structures; very few errors in agreement, number, verb tenses or moods, word order, articles, pronouns, prepositions.
VOCABULARY AND MECHANICS Excellent to very good complex range; accurate word/idiom choice; mastery of word forms and expressions; appropriate level of usage. Accurate spelling and use of diacritics (accent marks) in French.
