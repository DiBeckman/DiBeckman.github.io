Here is what I would have explained in class:

1. When you are talking about Profession et nationalité you need to choose between
il / elle est and c'est

The simplest format is"

sujet+être+profession/nationalité

examples:

Elle est médecin. (She is a doctor.)
Je suis professeur. (I am a professor.)
Ils sont français. (They are French.)

Note: you don't use an article in this format in French. You would use this format to answer the question "Qui est-ce?"

2. You can also do something very similar using:

c'est + article + profession/nationalité

C'est un médecin. (He is a doctor.)
Ce sont des français. (They are French.)

In this format you do include the indefinite article. Although the sentences mean the same thing, in this case, you are answering a question such as "Quelle est sa profession? Quelle est sa nationalité?"

3. However, when you want to modify the noun with an adjective, then you have no choice. You must use the C'est+ article format.

C'est + article + nom + adjectif

Here are two examples:

Il est pompier.
Il est courageux.
C'est un pompier courageux.


Elle est québécoise.

Elle est chanteuse.
C'est une chanteuse québécoise.

4. So, when you are just making a simple statement like He's a fireman or She's a singer, you can use the Il/Elle est+ nom+adjectif format.

When you combine them, you need to use the longer form: C'est un pompier courageux. C'est une chanteuse québécoise.

Your travail de classe (which is now un travail chez soi) gives you the opportunity to learn these grammar points by making your own sentences.

5. Assignment: Find two pictures of people where you can describe them and their profession. For each picture write 3 sentences, according to the model above. It would be lovely if you could include the picture as well.

In the first two sentences, tell the person's profession and attribute.
Modèle: Il est avocat. Il est sérieux.
Then in the third sentence, combine them and use the C'est un/une format
Modèle: C'est un avocat sérieux.

It's possible that you would use an adjective that comes before the noun: C'est un bon avocat.

6. The city and the verb aller + the preposition à

aller = to go
je vais nous allons
tu vas vous allez
il, elle, on va ils vont

aller prefers to be followed by the preposition à. Like the prepostion de, it combines with the definite article:

la préposition à:

la préposition l'article  
à + la à la
à + le au
à + l' à l'
à + les aux

Examples:

Le mécanicien va au garage.

Les serveuses vont au café.

Le médecin va à l'hôpital.

L'avocate va à la banque.
