In the year 2302 the Apocalypse occurred much like the book of Revelation foretold. Beasts scoured the earth. Famine, death, pestilence, and war riddled the Earth until life was no more. The Horsemen rode to the four corners of the Earth[1] in preparation for the grand finale. The Lamb’s Book of Life was opened by one who was left out of the Bible, but not mythology. This being was the Grim Reaper, the one who marked souls for their eternal destination. Earth was no more; the creations of God were either walking with Him or suffering in the lake of fire with Lucifer. The creations of individuals also were given eternal redemption or damnation. The rings of hell were many, and one of those rings was reserved for those characters who were given no redemption by their authors. Inside this ring a musical is about to take place. A musical on the vilest of stages – this is Literature on Lava.  

Act One

PRODUCER     [Nervously.] We cannot be late, this is not a rehearsal! She really has a problem with authority; you would think people would change. The gates will be opening anytime now.

            [HEDDA GABLER comes in from the left side of the stage. The most obvious          things about her are the gaping holes in her temple and back of the head, which     occasionally flow like a never-ending sanguine fountain. These wounds are her       eternal reminder of her suicide[2]. She is dressed in a somewhat loose-fitting     evening gown.]

HEDDA GABLER     The show can begin now. [Calling the STAGE MANAGER, she points to a tiny Pomeranian dog.] Get this pest away from me!

PRODUCER  Like it or not, the dog is part of the play.

            [The gates open and the brimstone pews are filled. The PRODUCER glances          from the stage to see who is in the audience.]

PRODUCER     Some quite unexpected guests are with us. I never expected to see a double dose of Death. Then again, I never expected being a character in a play could have eternal repercussions. Everyone breathe! This is it, folks, it’s show time!

            [Among the audience members are the GRIM REAPER and DEATH. The GRIM     REAPER is bearing his famous mirrored scythe and black frock while DEATH is         dressed in pale fashionable garments. DEATH is one of the Four Horsemen of the             Apocalypse.]

GRIM REAPER     [Hazily.] I never will understand it. We look completely different and have different jobs, yet as many worlds we see progress, the inhabitants always seem to confuse us.

DEATH     That is because you aren’t mentioned in the holiest book of all.

GRIM REAPER     Second holiest book of all, you mean. The holiest and most important book is and always will be in my possession, you know that. Where is the rest of your team, anyway? Did their horses run away again?

DEATH     [Angrily.] You know I never do any extracurricular activities with them. We turn circles into squares, and then I do not see them again until we do it again. Besides, they don’t necessarily enjoy my company. I think they’re jealous.

GRIM REAPER     [Laughing.] Jealous because they were given no names[3]? At least they were in the Bible, I had to get my name out there without any advertising. How

exactly do you go about turning circles into squares, anyway?  

DEATH     I’ll tell you about it sometime; it is quite perplexing and very awesome to say the least. The show is about to start. This Hedda Gabler sure is something from what I’ve heard. They say in her last performance her head began to bleed so profusely that she slipped in a puddle of her own blood. It created somewhat of a scandal, something she has always been afraid of[4] since Henrik Ibsen made her that way. These authors were so isolated[5] and focused on the unfortunate that their creative offspring only knew evil, and were given absolutely no chance of redemption. It is almost a shame, really.

            [The GRIM REAPER and DEATH laugh quietly as the surrounding fires dim. A      large, bright, and silent fire appears in the center of the stage for the        CHARACTERS to perform their songs. HEDDA GABLER comes in from the right side of the stage and stands in front of the blaze. Her eyes appear to glow red and            she begins to softly sing. Piano keys began to play slowly, as the opening act is on         way.]

HEDDA GABLER     Once upon a time I had it all. I had it all, I had it all. I had a husband, I had a house. I had maids, I had allure. I had wealth, I had it all. I had it all, I had it all. [She lets out a sigh, as the music speeds up.] I wanted so much more, I wanted something I could never have. I wanted to be a man, like my father, the alpha-male, the cock of the walk. I wanted to be…

            [JUDGE BRACK enters from behind the fire and interrupts HEDDA GABLER.      He stares HEDDA GABLER in the eyes and begins to sing forcefully.  The             music changes from piano to saxophone. HEDDA GABLER retreats to the audience’s right and remains on the stage, but out of sight.]

JUDGE BRACK     ME! You wanted to be me! You wanted to be the man! Not just a normal man, but a judge! I had power, I had control. I had what you wanted but you had what I wanted. [He lets out a sigh, as the music speeds up.] I wanted so much more, I wanted something I never did have. I wanted to be happily married, like my father, the happiest man I knew, the king of his domain. I wanted to be…

            [Another fire lights up behind HEDDA GABLER. JUDGE BRACK moves to the      audience’s left while the central fire follows him. Two fires are burning at an    equal distance and both CHARACTERS began to sing together. The music              reaches climax.]

HEDDA GABLER and JUDGE BRACK     YOU! I wanted to be you!  I longed for something impossible. I had it all, I had it all, but I didn’t want any of it. I wanted the one thing I knew I could not have.

            [Both CHARACTERS face each other and walk towards one another, the fires         follow them as they make their ways back to the center of the stage.]

HEDDA GABLER     I wanted another life, I wanted your life. I wanted it all, I wanted it all. Just another gender and I could have been complete.

JUDGE BRACK     I wanted someone in my life, I wanted your life. I wanted it all, I wanted it all. Just another person and I could have been complete.

HEDDA GABLER     Who said my marriage was happy?

JUDGE BRACK     Who said my life was happy? May I have this dance?

            [JUDGE BRACK extends his hand. His hand is left dangling]

HEDDA GABLER     I have said it before to you, Judge, I am all danced out[6].    

HEDDA GABLER and JUDGE BRACK     [Grieving.] We only needed one more thing!    

            [The final notes linger as the two fires become one large flame. The blood from       HEDDA GABLER’s head glistens on the stage floor and both CHARACTERS            exit through the flame. The audience is left with the smell of burned flesh and raw    blood. No applause is      given. The CHARACTERS go backstage.]

GRIM REAPER     You would be surprised how many souls get the low swing of the scythe merely because they did not have one more thing.

DEATH     The low swing of the scythe?

GRIM REAPER     That is how I mark the souls. A mark below the middle means hell; a mark above the middle means heaven.

DEATH     What if you mark the middle? [Taking out his program.]

GRIM REAPER     [Sighing.] I do not make mistakes. That is why both God and Lucifer allow me to direct the souls. Who is up next?

Act 2

DEATH     Hermann and Lisaveta are next. Alexander Pushkin made it clear that Hermann was not to get redemption but poor Lisaveta received collateral damnation. Her life started out bad and even after marriage her life was still poor[7].

GRIM REAPER     They died the exact same day; I remember when I marked them. Their souls were very close to one another. It is strange how things work out like that. Even if life separates people, death can bring them back together. Look at them now.

            [LISAVATE and HERMANN enter the stage. LISAVETA is dressed in rags and       HERMANN is wrapped in a straightjacket with his hands crossed and shoulders        almost touching. The music changes from a slow violin when LISAVETA sings        to a strange mix of string and brass instruments when HERMANN sings.]

LISAVETA     Once upon a time I fell in love and it was sublime. The first moment you stood on street and stared[8] I admit I was scared. What was this sensation? You knew of my hesitation. My heart was racing the moment you handed me the first letter[9] I knew things would not get any better. I tried to make them go away but you sent me letters every day[10]. My heart was anxious for the first occasion and I was unable to stop your Napoleonic invasion. Hermann, Hermann, Hermann, I lived a life of hardship and I never escaped your swooning grip. Hermann, Hermann, Hermann…

            [HERMANN shuffles to the center of the stage. The fire dims so the audience can     only see a silhouette of a man bound in a straightjacket, occasionally twitching about the stage. LISAVETA stands a distance from the fire and tries to take her      eyes off HERMANN to no avail.]

HERMANN     Three, seven, ace! Three, seven, queen! This is worse than Room Seventeen[11]! Oh, my hell I thought I had left with my last breath! I tied the noose just as the Countess commanded. Now here I am eternally stranded. That old witch! She was filthy rich! I would stop her heart again if I could; all the demons that were once in my head and now the listeners here, know that I would! The game of life was played but misery held all the cards. Three, seven, ace! Three, seven, queen!

LISAVETA     Hermann, Hermann, Hermann, you truly are a monster but now you are mine. Our spirits will eternally intertwine.

HERMANN     I used your heart to command your head! Three, seven, ace! Three, seven, queen!

            [The fire brightens and reveals HERMANN fleeing the stage, twitching the entire     way until he is out of sight. LISAVETA remains on the stage facing away from the       audience as the fire returns to the previous dim state.]

LISAVETA     Hermann, Hermann, Hermann, please come back my dear. At least take the secret staircase, my beloved Engineer. Cherished Hermann chose money over my affection[12]. He crucified my heart and this is the resurrection.

            [HERMANN reenters the stage with a hand-crafted rope around his neck. The fire intensifies so show the audience of his most recent unsuccessful suicide.]

HERMANN     Let me out of this routine! Allow my revenge upon the queen! Why is she not damned to hell!? Three, seven, ace! Three, seven, queen! Where else would she dwell!? 

            [HERMANN again runs off the stage, LISAVETA follows him as they both           ascend the red glowing stairs to the backstage. No applause is given.]

DEATH     Did you really allow Countess *** entrance into heaven?

GRIM REAPER     I don’t allow anyone to go anywhere, those rules are beyond me. I marked her to hell once and then I marked her to heaven shortly after. She must have made a deal during her short time in hell. She did know a very powerful secret…

DEATH     Lucifer will gladly lose one soul to gain two. The proof is all around us, look how many more souls rest in here. There are so many here that hell is broken into different rings just to hold them all.

GRIM REAPER     Do we have another duet up next?

 

 

Act 3

DEATH     It’s a trio of Emma Bovary, Rodolphe Boulanger, and Monsieur Lheureux. Gustave. Flaubert may as well have been employed by Lucifer; he sent more characters to hell in this single story than most authors have in their entire writing career. Another example of how much easier it is to concentrate on the evils of the world.

            [The three CHRACTERS enter the stage. RODOLPHE stands to the left of the         audience. LHEUREUX stands to the right of the audience. EMMA stands between        the two men. Three fires blaze behind them. When a CHARACTER sings, their fire lights up brighter than the other two flames. An instrumental with bell and whistle       instruments begins to resonate throughout.]

RODOLPHE     Once upon a time I was a connoisseur of dames[13].

EMMA     I thought my life was hard, now I am engulfed in flames.

LHEUREUX     I took financial advantage of those with no brains.    

RODOLPHE     I loved to say my hellos but I always planned out my goodbyes.

EMMA     My boredom and unhappiness lead to my untimely demise.

LHEUREUX     I would drive them into debt and no one was wise.

RODOLPHE     I could have every one of them and they all fell for me.

EMMA     All I wanted was to escape the bourgeoisie.

LHEUREUX     From curtains to carpet, it’s a deal I guarantee!

RODOLPHE     Love them and leave them was always my foundation.

EMMA     All I wanted was another living situation.

LHEUREUX     Buy now, layaway; I will come later for my compensation.

 

            [The middle flame begins to burn wildly. The weaves of EMMA’S dress became      noticeable, the pores of her white skin shine, and even her eyelids were visible.       The harsh glare removes RODOLPHE and LHEUREUX from the audience’s        view. The great flow of red surrounds EMMA[14]. A flute begins to play]

EMMA     My dreams, my wonderful dreams, my dreams controlled me.  They were vivid dreams of clarity that only I could desire and understand. My dreams, my sweet dreams, my dreams compelled me. They were glorious dreams of the impossible that only I could chase and achieve. My dreams, my soothing dreams, my dreams gripped me. They were merely dreams of a fictitious reality that only I could only yearn and crave. My dreams, my deceitful dreams, my dreams detached me. They were narcissistic dreams of conceit that only I could conceive and project. My dreams, my dreadful dreams, my dreams pushed me. They were convincing dreams of ambition that only I could make possible and real. My dreams, my self-destructive dreams, my dreams destroyed me. They were possessed dreams of envy that only I could hunger and thirst. My reality, my discontented reality, my reality blinded me. It was an unforgiving reality that only I could face and stomach. My arsenic, my redemptive arsenic, my arsenic suffocated me. It was nauseating arsenic that only I could enjoy and hate. This hell, this atrocious hell, this hell consumed me. It is a tormenting hell that only I could predict and deserve!

            [The flute stops and the vicious middle flame engulfs the two smaller surrounding   flames along with EMMA, RODOLPHE and LHEUREUX. The audience is again   left with the familiar smell of burning flesh. The flame continues to spread and        stage is destroyed. The audience flees. The play is in intermittence until the   stage is repaired. No applause is given.]

Intermission

GRIM REAPER     It seems we have some time until the play resumes. What a disaster.

DEATH     It was certainly not in the program. Disaster is pretty common around here though, it has to be expected by now.

GRIM REAPER     Maybe to the inhabitants here but not to the guests, we should leave.

DEATH     And do what? We are both on hiatus until God creates another planet full of ungrateful species who deny Him every chance they get. I do love the look on their faces when I ride by and they realize that they were wrong.

GRIM REAPER     Some have to be ungrateful; otherwise I would be out of a job. Now that we have time, how exactly do you go about ending worlds?

DEATH     Ah, yes, turning circles into squares as I call it. The moment the Horsemen are released from our seals[15], the world stops rotating, which causes major natural disasters. Each Horseman takes a corner of the world - North, South, East, and West, all more or less of an equal distance from each other. The entire planet is then compressed and loses its shape because gravity no longer pulls it, we create our own force. After the compression, our horses drag the corners and stretch the world out until we are exactly equal distances from each other to create a perfect square. The water is already evaporated at this point and all that is left is an enormous green and brown square.

GRIM REAPER     Are the colors spread out chaotically or is there order?

DEATH     There is perfect order. Think of is as a perfect black and white marble floor, the brown and green colors are that precise. They are all miniature squares inside of a large square laid in order, brown, green, brown, green, etc. The green squares then vanish into the air starting from one corner and ending at its diagonal corner.

GRIM REAPER     [Puzzled.] Where do the squares go?

DEATH     [Ignoring the question at first.] Then the brown squares disappear in a similar fashion, the squares disappear from corner to corner, diagonally. Then our job is done. A new world is then created from the old pieces; this new world has no water[16].

GRIM REAPER     Then my job kicks into overtime. All those souls, it is nice to have the Book of Life with me. What happens to all those waterless planets?

DEATH     They remain, and their traces of water can still be discovered, but liquid water will never be found on them. There can only be liquid water on one planet at a time, God really does have a fascination with it, and it gives the mortals greater free will since it is logical to assume that all life needs water. Once they assume this, they no longer need to believe that a higher power created them.

GRIM REAPER     And that means I will always have a job.

            [A loud voice is heard from behind DEATH and the GRIM REAPER. This is the      voice of the PRODUCER.]

Act 4

PRODUCER     [Screaming.] Everyone! Everyone, please come back in! We have everything back in place and it is safe. Thank you all for your patience, it will be worth the wait I assure you.

            [The audience returns to their brimstone pews. The stage no longer has fires but    instead a stream of molten lava flows through the back of the stage. The lava            covers the entire stage and slowly is eating the stage away. New programs are        given to the audience.]

DEATH     It seems as though they realize the stage is slowly being consumed.

GRIM REAPER     Why do you say that, and who is up next?

DEATH     They have merged performances together. Performances that were originally suppose to be solos are now duets and trios. Gustav and Gregory are next. Thomas Mann created Gustav in his own image, now they are both in hell. Frank Kafka tried to give Gregory redemption and some people believe he succeeded but it was too late to redeem Gregory after the second paragraph in the story[17]. This should be interesting.

            [GUSTAV enters the stage. He is a somewhat short and stocky man. His hair is      dark and slick and his glasses reflect the flowing magma behind him. GREGORY         enters the stage. GREGORY is a large, hideous bug and walks very slow. There is an object logged into his back that has shattered his exoskeleton and is now    completely covered in pus. In the front row of the audience sits a horribly             disfigured little boy, no older than fourteen and an older man dressed in a  commissionaire’s uniform. The pipe organ begins to play dirge-like music]

GUSTAV     [In a scratchy voice.] Once upon a time, the signs told me to refuse. “Refuse” said the apocalyptic beasts[18] and my common sense also wanted to say no; but my author made me go. Go to Venice and enjoy your immorality. Immorality despite the afterlife was not my key to living or writing. Writing under my banner of “Despite[19]” should have had limits. Limits my author did not know. Know this Hermes – your smiles[20] were drawn on by your author, the Author of Evil. Your Lucifer is my Thomas Mann and now we are both aflame with them.

            [GREGORY slowly crawls near GUSTAV to steal the glowing light. GREGORY     has no voice and instead communicates through subtitles. His somewhat human          looking lips move and his words are visually displayed from his mouth for the         audience to read. The words stay for only a few moments until they disperse into         billows of smoke. No music is played.]

GREGORY     Once upon a time I could sell you mostly anything, but my author sold my soul. I would wake up and work everyday, but one day I woke up a creature. I was afraid to tell my family, as they all told me to open my door. Soon no one could understand me, and not even I understood the metamorphosis. Don’t you see everyone, what you saw in the story was not me.

            [A small, round object is thrown from the audience directly at GREGORY. The       object strikes the side of his neck and becomes lodged inside of him. GUSTAV           begins to sing and the pipe organ begins to play again.]

GUSTAV     Sweet art, sweet nature, why was I forced to fuse the two together? If writing is merely an extension of self, does that really make me deserving of this place? Why was I unable to keep my enthusiasm to the level of appreciation and not obsession[21]? Why was I left to fantasize about a boy whose beauty was the paragon of perfection? Why were his outfits gorgeous and his smile so sweet? Why, why, why, did I not have a chance to resist? Why only now do I realize why his bows were red his teeth anemic[22]? Now I realize how I could not resist, for I loved him. I loved everything about him and if I had the chance to return to Venice and do the same thing I surely would.

            [The disfigured boy jumps onto the stage and lunges towards GUSTAV. GUSTAV  is startled and stumbles backwards. The little boy makes contact with GUSTAV

            by jumping into his arms. This momentum forces both CHARACTERS to fall

into the hot lava that runs through the stage. No applause is given.]

DEATH     That is a lawsuit waiting to happen.

GRIM REAPER     Good thing the lawyer ring of hell is closed off completely. Next?

Act 5

DEATH     Looks like Gabriel and Judith Shakespeare are up next. Author James Joyce made Gabriel’s last days filled with unequivocal envy. Gabriel soon took matters into his own hands and took his own life. The author Virginia Woolf, while trying to make a point about the unfairness of gender roles, ended Judith’s life prematurely and without redemption.

            [GABRIEL and JUDITH take the stage. GABRIEL’S body is charred and    occasionally small fires are noticeable on him. JUDITH’S body is blue and she is     constantly shivering[23]. JUDITH stands close to GABRIEL. Fast drums play when   GABRIEL sings while a harp plays for JUDITH.]

GABRIEL     Once upon a time I was the life of the party. I was above the rest until he knocked me down. His dead soul made my blood boil. That song, I will never forget that song[24] for it destroyed my life. I never should have asked my wife Gretta what she was thinking. I thought she was thinking about me, but instead she was thinking…

JUDITH SHAKESPEARE     [Interrupting.] O’ Michael, Michael! Wherefore art thou Michael?

GABRIEL     Michael Furey was the young boy who stole Gretta’s heart before I had a chance. They were smitten and then they separated. He visited her on a cold winter’s night. He died from the cold and she never let him go!

JUDITH SHAKESPEARE     Some Cupid kill with arrows, some with freezing rain.

GABRIEL     After my epiphany[25] I collected my thoughts and knew what I had to do. The only way to steal her love back was to die as Michael Furey died. Such a name, Michael Furey!

JUDITH SHAKESPEARE     What’s in a name? That which we call a skunk by any other name would stink just as acrid.

GABRIEL     I would not die from the cold, oh no, my rage and passion were too great. Fire! I would set myself on fire! In front of her to show her that my love burns for her hotter than any flame and in the end she will love me the most!

            [The stage creeks as the magma spills backstage forcing the CHARACTERS            backstage to retreat. A tiny Pomeranian dog runs across the stage barking.]

JUDITH SHAKESPEARE     A dog! A dog! My life for a dog! Out, damned dog! Out I say!

GABRIEL     That warm summer night I displayed my love for her in an unmistakable way! Her screams showed proved that she did love me! I will never forget those screams of affection. She loved me from the moment my body was consumed by the flames and she will love me…

JUDITH SHAKESPEARE       Tomorrow, and tomorrow, and tomorrow, her love keeps at a steady pace from day to day.

GABRIEL     She will never love Michael Furey as much as she loved me, even if they are together for eternity in heaven. My death was marvelous and now I must take my leave.

JUDITH SHAKESPEARE       Goodbye, goodbye! Parting is such bitter joy.

            [GABRIEL and JUDITH walk backstage then quickly turn around and walk off       the stage towards the audience. The back of the stage is completely inundated with lava. The lava is making its way towards the front of the stage and       audience. No applause is given.]

GRIM REAPER     This is getting ridiculous.

DEATH     We should have gone to the play in heaven. The stages never break and dogs don’t randomly interrupt performances.

GRIM REAPER     It also smells better there, are we almost done here?

Act 6

DEATH     This is the last act. Candide, Pangloss, and Wong are the last remaining souls. Voltaire was a master of satire, unfortunately he created Candide to never be content with anything and Pangloss content with the worst of things. As for Wong, his author, Bertolt Brecht, made him believe in false gods and also made him a thief. His death was rather deserved.

            [CANDIDE and PANGLOSS enter from the front of the stage and turn around to   the audience. CANDIDE is dressed like a farmer while PANGLOSS is dressed in          fancy clothes without proof that his hands were ever used for work. WONG             enters the stage holding a beggar’s mug, and is dressed in ripped clothes.       Cymbals play for CANDIDE and PANGLOSS. Deep cellos play for WONG. ]     

CANDIDE     Once upon a time I lived a life filled with ups and downs. The good times were never good enough and the bad times were never really that bad.

PANGLOSS     It was the best of all possible worlds and to think otherwise would be plain foolish.

CANDIDE     [Visibly angry at PANGLOSS.] Always believing that motto and making sure I heard it, even when poor Cunégonde’s heart stopped you made sure I heard those words. She enslaved herself to her own pastries[26] and loved them more than she loved me, how is that the best possible outcome?

PANGLOSS     [Shocked.] These are not your lines, boy. I shall still address your question. I said it once before, everything is meant to serve an end and not just any end, but the best end possible[27].

CANDIDE     [Outraged.] And now we are eternally tormented in hell, how could this get any better? Tell me wise one!

PANGLOSS     It could be worse. We could be with the lawyers. If you were with Cunégonde imagine how unhealthy you would be eating all those pastries. We have our health and should be thankful for that.

CANDIDE     I have my health but your face looks as though it is devouring itself from the syphilis[28]  you contracted.

            [The stage collapses while lava begins to consume it. The lava flows near the          CHARACTERS on-stage, forcing them to find dry places to stand. WONG rushes    to the front of the stage.]

WONG     Once upon a time I lived a life of hardship and poverty. I still believed in the gods and communicated with the regularly. How was a poor water merchant to know the gods were false? As false as the bottom on my water cup[29]. After the gods left me, there was a severe drought and I traveled a long distance in search of water[30]. I did not find a drop. As I was returning to Setzuan I occasionally met with other travelers who were so kind to drop a few pieces of silver into my cup. I was exhausted to the point of death when I arrived at Setzuan and immediately found the nearest store. I tried to purchase a drink and some cigarettes and as I reached into my cup to pay, the money was gone. The bottom was up the whole time and my silver was scattered somewhere in the outskirts of Setzuan. The owner of the store refused my business and I was thrown out. I traveled back in search of my silver but it was too late. My mouth was as dry as the earth I was walking on. I was a water merchant and I died of dehydration. I will never taste water again.

            [WONG is too weary to move from the incoming magma and is swept away in a

            violent current. CANDIDE and PANGLOSS stand on a small section in the            center of the stage that has not been touched by the lava.]

CANDIDE     Pangloss, you stupid fool! How did you become an instructor and why did I listen to your utter falsehoods? This surely is not the best of all worlds! You definitely are all tongue.

PANGLOSS     Candide, I am no fool! I studied the metaphysico-theologico-cosmo…

CANDIDE     Loony! You have always been insane, why I ever listened to you I shall never know, but I will never listen to you again.

PANGLOSS     If that is your choice I shall say that it is the best of all…

            [CANDIDE shoves PANGLOSS into the nearby lava and steps across his body to   flee from the stage. The entire stage is a large pool of lava. The audience begins       to leave. No applause is given.]

DEATH     That was sheer disaster.

GRIM REAPER     This place isn’t known for pleasant things.

DEATH     Do we still have time to catch the play in heaven?

GRIM REAPER     I am no longer in the mood for plays and I don’t think I will be for a long time.

DEATH     [To the audience.] Writing serves a greater purpose than people give it credit for. The words on paper can lead a man towards redemption or lead that man towards damnation. Be careful what you write because when I come, I do not bring salvation. I bring death.

            [Fade to red.]
