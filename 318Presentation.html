You may also choose another French film that is not on either of these lists Sign up for your films on a first-come, first served basis by an email to me. You must make your choice by March 25. La Structure de la Présentation

  • De quel genre de film s'agit-il?
  • Résumez l'intrigue du film. Expliquez la structure et les thèmes du film et notez le vocabulaire essentiel ou spécifique au film.
  • Décrivez les personnages principaux.
  • Choisissez une scène et commentez cette scène et sa technique cinématographique.
  • Comment est-ce que ce film fait partie de l'héritage du cinéma français?
  • Qu'est-ce que vous avez apprécié le plus dans le film?

Préparez une fiche pédagogique pour le professeur avec le titre du film, le réalisateur, les personnages et acteurs principaux, notes sur le vocabulaire, la structure, les thèmes, et les sources consultées.

Project: For your final project you may choose to write a paper in French (7-10 pages) or make your own film in French (5 minutes). You may also choose to work with a partner for the paper or with a group for the film. For your paper, you may choose to write about the film you present in class or another French film. I am flexible about the precise topic: please choose something that you find compelling. The paper can follow the model of the chapters on each film in Singerman (without the dossier critique), the model of Corrigan's "Critical Essay" (12-14; with sample essays 78-81; 96-102; 150-154), or focus on a particular actor, director, or other film-related topic. The peer review draft of your term project is due April 8. For the peer review, you should bring two copies of the first page and some form of outline of your paper along with two copies of the peer review form for your classmates. If you are making a film, you will be responsible for turning in a written work in French (5-7 pages) as well as the film. The project should include a scenario as well as an artist's journal. In your journal, please reflect (in French) on the different stages of your project, reflecting your own individual role.What did you learn about filmmaking from this project? How did learning about French film impact your work. The complete rough draft is due on the day of your presentation, April 17-24. The final project will be due Monday, April 29, University Reading Day in the main FLL Office, Withers 310. For full credit, you must turn in your final draft along with your peer review draft, your rough draft and your Pledge, Checklist, and Reflection Form. Late submissions will only be accepted with my permission, and must be arranged before the due date of the paper. Otherwise, you will lose 10 points each day that the assignment is late.

The Pledge, Checklist and Reflection Form also includes the Scoring Rubric for the term project which shows how your papers will be graded and what to strive for in the areas of content, organization and format, grammar, vocabulary and fluency. A Short Guide to Writing About Film Chapters 5-7 includes details about what is expected for style, research and manuscipt form.
