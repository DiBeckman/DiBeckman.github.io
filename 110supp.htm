Adjectives

Before a plural adjective which precedes a noun, des usually becomes de. If the adjective comes after the noun, des does not change to de.
Ce sont des amis fidèles.
Ce sont de bons amis.

"Fickle"adjectives: "Most French adjectives must be placed after the noun they modify, except for the BAGS exceptions. There are also a number of French adjectives which have different meanings depending on where they are placed. Generally speaking, when the adjective precedes the noun, it has a figurative or subjective meaning, whereas the adjective which follows the noun has a literal or objective meaning." Eg: Ancien, bon, brave. . . ." http://french.about.com/od/grammar/a/adjectives_fickle.htm

The situtations where adjectives don't need to agree: see http://french.about.com/od/grammar/a/adjectives_inv.htm#
