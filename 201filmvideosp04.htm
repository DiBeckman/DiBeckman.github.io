VIDEOS: You will be required to choose at least three cultural videos to view during the semester. All videos are on reserve at the Media Center on the second floor of the Erdahl-Cloyd Wing of the NC State Library. Each week, one video (or set of two videos) will be shown at 4 PM on Monday and Tuesday. You may go to see the video(s) at that time. If you are unable to see the video(s) at the scheduled time, you may be able to arrange for a personal viewing at another time in the Media Center.

After you have seen the video(s), submit a one-page, typed commentary in French. (That is, one page for each video or set of 2 videos. In the case of the latter, your commentary will be 1/2 page long for each video.) Please do not summarize the video. You may discuss what intrigued or surprised you about the cultural content, what you thought about the content from an American perspective, how the video content fits with your conception of the French culture, etc. See the Grading Rubric for Video Commentaries for more information. If you are not sure what to write about cultural content, the following pages may be helpful: An Introduction to Some Basic Ideas about Intercultural Communication and sample commentaries from my students in Fall 2003.

Please note: You may choose any three (or more) videos (or sets of videos) according to your interest. You may hand in your typed commentaries any time following the showing of the video. However, you must hand in at least two videos commentaries by Thursday February 26. The last video commentary must be handed in by Thursday April 15. See the schedule below for dates and titles of videos.

VIDEOS FOR FLF 201: Spring 2004

January 26-27. Chanel, Chanel (English, 60 minutes) The world of fashion has produced no more charismatic figure than Coco Chanel, the famous fashion designer whose sense of style, and simplicity and ease of clothing became her hallmarks. Using rare archival footage, this program explores the course of her career as well as the fascinating story of her personal life.

February 2-3: Edouard Manet: painter of Modern Life. (English, 27 minutes). This visually powerful documentary presents the great French impressionist as a daring observer of the 19th century urban scene. It examines Manet's major paintings using the artist's own words and those of his contemporaries. and Berthe Morisot: The Forgotten Impressionist (English, 32 minutes) Although she was at the center of the Parisian avant-garde painting and influenced Renoir, Manet, and Degas, when she died, her death certificate listed her as having "no profession". An interesting biography of a painter whose talent was often overshadowed by her womanhood. (Read p. 61 and p. 64 before seeing the video.)

February 9-10: Memories of Monet (English, 29 minutes) This is an award winning documentary that blends supreb views of Monet's Gardens at Giverny with his paintings. The story is told in the words of Lilla Cabot Perry, an American artist who was a friend of Monet. It includes very interesting information about some lesser-known American Impressionist painters: Robinson, Breck, Butler, and Frieske. and Pierre-Auguste Renoir (English, 26 minutes) Renoir was intrigued by the female form. His sensual paintings of women highlight this video celebrating his life and art. (Note: there is some nudity in this video.)

February 16-17: Van Gogh Biography (English, 45 minutes) The story of the mad genius of painting including his famed run-in with his ear! Though he is now worldfamous, he sold only one canvas in his lifetime. Hear Van Gogh's own words - taken from his letters to his brother - as he explains his life and work. and Surprised!: The Paintings of Henri Rousseau (English, 21 minutes) "The paintings of Henri Rousseau invite us into a haunting imaginary world, a tapestry of patterns and colors that is both illogical and highly convincing." As a Parisian toll collector with questionable technique, Rousseau's primitive style had a substantial impact on the avant-garde movement. This is the story of his life and art.

February 23-24: The Biography of Picasso (English, 90 minutes) A fascinating look at the life and work of the most influential artist of the 20th century. Intriguing parallels between his life and his art.

Note: The film listed below may be substituted for any of the above. It is available at video rental stores as well as the Media Center.

Camille Claudel (French with English subtitles, 159 minutes -a feature-length film) A historically accurate depiction of two great sculptors of modern art: Camille Claudel and Auguste Rodin. Both an inspiring saga of artistic vision and the haunting story of a doomed romance, it offers a brilliant study of the thin line separating creativity, passion, and madness.(Several scenes of nude models.) (Read p. 65 before viewing the video.)

March 1-2: The Mystery of Picasso (Some French, mostly music, 77 minutes) Experience the mysterious connection between the artist's vision and its realization on canvas as Picasso creates over 15 works before your very eyes. These unique creations were destroyed after the filming!

March 15-16: The Biography of Joan of Arc (English, 50 minutes) The complete story of the "Maid of Orleans" who was burned as a heretic and became a saint.

March 22-23: Versailles (English, 56 mintues) This video relates the complete story of the magnificent château of Versailles, the glorious palace of Louis XIV, the "Sun King" and of his descendants, Louis XV and Louis XVI. The narration tells the stories of the lives of the kings and queens whom we associate with the royal apartments, the Hall of Mirrors, Louis XV's desk (a masterpiece of French cabinet work), Marie Antoinette's bedroom (as it was when she set off on her way to the guillotine), the opera house, the chapel, the spectacular gardens, and more.
