Updated December 4, 2013

  • Read across the chart to see the assignments due that day. Module and page numbers in columns 2, 3, and 4 refer to the textbook; page numbers in the final column refer to the Workbook/Lab Manual.
  • Assignments in the white and green pages in the textbook (columns 2, 3, and 4) should be completed before coming to class. Homework should be turned in at the beginning of class.
  • Compositions, one per module, are listed in the second column and should be turned in on the date indicated. All compositions will be revised by you based on my corrections, and turned in again within the next week. Beginning with Module 3, I will give a numerical grade on revised compositions.
  • The third column indicates reading exercises in the white pages to turn in with other daily written homework.
  • The fourth column lists the written exercises in the green pages to be completed and corrected in a different color using Appendix B, p. 485 ff. in the textbook.
  • The final column lists pages in the Workbook/Lab Manual. The Activités Écrites [written exercises] (pp. 1-96) are listed first and Actvités de compréhension et de prononication [listening exercises] (pp. 143-204) are listed second. The audio for the workbook is delivered via the Cengage website http://login.cengagebrain.com. It is also available on our Moodle site: https://wolfware.ncsu.edu/ Correct your work using the Workbook Answer Key which is posted on our Moodle site. YOU are responsible for keeping up with the workbook exercises corresponding to the material covered in class. Bring your completed, corrected, work to class on exam days to receive full credit.
  • You are also responsible for keeping up with our second textbook English Grammar for Students of French (EGSF). Assigned chapters are listed below on the syllabus in conjunction with each exam. Use the French_review_Booklet.pdf to find both the exercises and answers. Some chapters do not have associated exercises. Bring your EGSF work to class on exam days, too, in order to receive full credit for completed, corrected exercises.
