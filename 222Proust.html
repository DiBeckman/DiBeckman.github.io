Proust

The different "movements" of the overture, according to page numbers in our anthology:

1813-1817: Disorientation: all the places he would wake up, including Combray.

1817-1826: Combray in general and Swann.

1826-1837: One of Swann’s visits in particular.

1837-1841:  Reading scene: very important to Marcel’s decision to become a writer. His initiation into literature—a rite of passage

1841-1844: Madeleine scene; involuntary memory, lost time recaptured.
