"Five Stages of Grief", a model introduced by Elisabeth Kübler-Ross in her 1969 book On Death and Dying.
The stages are:
Denial: "I feel fine."; "This can't be happening." 'Not to me!"
Anger: "Why me? It's not fair!"
Bargaining: "Just let me live to see my children graduate."
Depression: "I'm so sad, why bother with anything?"; "I'm going to die . . . What's the point?"
Acceptance:
"It's going to be OK."; "I can't fight it, I may as well prepare for it."
