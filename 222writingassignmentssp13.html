    Class 16 (Flaubert 5) due Wednesday, February 13

    Finish reading Madame Bovary 1215-1249.

    Writing assignments:

    1. Finish your character sketch to turn in. What happens to your character(s) in Book III?
    2. Choose any one of the following questions to answer and include the question with your answer. These questions would also inspire some good discussions in your Moodle posts.

      3. FORMALIST CRITICISM:

      • Flaubert changes the tempo of Madame Bovary by describing certain events in great detail, others are briefly told. How does the form of Madame Bovary reflect this?
      • How does the binary opposition between Emma and Charles reenforce the moral of Madame Bovary?
      • Power is frequently defined as the ability to influence the behavior of people with or without resistance, as well as to act or say something in a certain way. In Flaubert's novel, is there a character that lacks this power? If so, describe both the character and how this loss of power affects this character throughout the story
      • What purpose does the intense and detailed descriptions serve? How does it reinforce and/or add meaning to the story?
      • How much would these characters be changed, if at all, if they were written by a Romantic author? Would the plot of the story be different or could Flaubert be closely related to a writer like Voltaire?

      SOCIOLOGICAL CRITICISM:

      Marxist Criticism:

      • Is Emma solely responsible for her problem, or is she a victim of society? Is it easy to sympathize with her? Why or why not?
      • What do Lheureux's actions say about the Capitalist economic system, and what does Emma and Charles' response to his actions show about Capitalism's effects on society?
      • Marxism is the social policies and principles proposed by Karl Marx around the time when Flaubert wrote Madame Bovary. What was the social status of women in this story? More specifically, how do men seem to shape and impact the lives of the women they are around; especially Emma?
      • What character epitomizes Flaubert's view on society and wordly objects, or his dislike of class separation and the bourgeoisie?

      Feminist Criticism:

      • Is Emma's assertion of her feminine autonomy an expression of a desire to be free from repression, or a show of typically perceived "feminine hysteria" culminating in her demise?
      • Do Emma's flawed romantic ideals come from being a woman in a male-dominated society? In other words, if Emma were born a man would she be able to live out her dreams instead of having to repress them for society's sake?
      • Does the author, Flaubert, depict Emma's personality as a harsh human being because of her gender?

      CULTURAL AND HISTORICAL CRITICISM:

      • How do Emma's fantasies of a cheating wife tell us about the cultural and historical references of the time?
      • What historical events influenced Flaubert's life and led to an influence in his writing of Madame Bovary? For example, why was he so against the middle-class and their values?
      • Is the character Emma a Romantic character or is she a Realistic character with Romantic hopes and dreams?
      • How would responses to this story differ if it were written in a modern day context? What if everything stayed the same but it was only written and released in the 2000s? Do you think that looking at it from these viewpoints help you understand better the context it was written in and the importance?
      • How does Flaubert's detailing of the different social classes Madame Bovary is a part of reflect his personal view of each?
      • How do the descriptions of not only characters and the scenes in Madame Bovary reflect the time period?

      PSYCHOLOGICAL CRITICISM:

      • What does Flaubert's portrayal of Emma's depression and discontent with her husband illustrate about the author's personality?
      • What do Emma's emotions and behaviors reveal about her psychological state? Do you think that she let her materialistic needs and desire for romanticism lead to a split personality?
      • Emma is obsessed with what she thinks she wants, and is permanently dissatisfied with what she has. This is a hallmark of traditional Freudian narcissism: an obsession with the self, but a complete lack of knowledge of self. Emma wants to be happy at the expense of actual happiness or the happiness of those around her. Q. Does Emma's lack of self-knowledge and obsession with her own happiness lead her to depression and the suffering of those around her?
      • Do you think that Emma's character was meant to raise awareness of bipolar disorder at this time? Could Flaubert have been on to something that was beyond his time?
      • Do you think Flaubert used Emma's character to reveal some of truth about his life or fantasies?
      • How does Emma's mental state reveal about her personalities and behaviors?
      • How do Emma's unrealistic dreams of romance lead to her suicide?
      • Do you think Emma is capable of real love and romance or do these only exist in her fantasized romantic world (given the fact that Emma is psychologically unstable)?
      • It says that the elder Madame Bovary was "rejoicing inwardly at the thought of recapturing [Charles'] affection" when Emma died. What does this say about how the author views mothers?
      • Could Emma's episodes of grandeur of Eros love be the product of a variance of an depersonalizing disorder caused by depression? Or could it be the product of a midlife crisis? Why or Why not? Use evidential support from Madame Bovary to support your answer.

      READER RESPONSE CRITICISM:

      • Can you see yourself living during this time period? What are some aspects of life you can see yourself doing/not doing during this time?
      • Do you find this more of a tragedy or a satire? How does this shape your response to the events in Madame Bovary?
      • When reading Madame Bovary, which character do you empathize with the most? Can you relate to them? Do you feel bad for them? Explain why you empathize with this character.
      • Many writings reflect the current happenings of a time. We know that Madame Bovary is making fun of romantic ideals; instead, it displays a more realist approach. At the time this writing was published, do you think many people could relate to this story (as far as infidelity, drama or characters go), or is this just a juicy story that catches attention? If it does relate to the public, how does it do so?

      ECOLOGICAL CRITICISM:

      • On page 1041 Flaubert writes, "The stream, which makes this part of Rouen a kind of squalid little Venice, flowed just below, stained yellow, purple or blue between its bridge and railings". How does Flaubert use imagery to show the affects of industrialization on nature in this literary work? Do you believe Flaubert has a negative opinion about the impacts on nature by mankind?
      • What roles do flowers and plants play in reflecting the inner conflicts of the characters?

      QUEER THEORY:

      • Would you agree that this novel contains an immense amount of sexuality? If so, would you agree or consider the fact that Emma may have a serious issue psychologically due to her personal and constant desires and needs for satisfaction in life through men and sex? (She really shows a never-ending dissatisfaction with her life; she constantly needs attention, luxurious items, men, sex, to feel good, to be accepted, to be rich, to be famous, to have the world at the palm of her hand, and so on.) Would you then agree that she may be narcissistic and may be infatuated with herself suggesting homosexuality?
      • Does heterosexuality necessarily lead to unhappiness?
      • What are the material and psychic conditions that lead a middle-class woman to find heterosexual marriage so unfulfilling?
      • Does compulsory heterosexuality inhibit some more polymorphous desire from being gratified?

    • Give concrete examples (quotes) from the texts to support your arguments, but not to fill up space. These quotations are the evidence you need to provide to prove your thesis. You will usually use at least one in each of body paragraphs.
