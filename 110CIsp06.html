LEARNING OUTCOMES: At the end of this course, students will be able to:
1. use learned grammatical structures to speak and write in elementary or novice-level French;
2. create written and oral sentences using learned vocabulary;
3. read French at the elementary or novice level;
4. understand spoken French at the elementary or novice level;
5. identify aspects of the cultures of the French-speaking world.

WELCOME TO FRENCH! This course aims at giving you a solid foundation in understanding, speaking, reading and writing the French language at the elementary level. To accomplish these goals, it is essential that we use French as the language of the classroom. Handy phrases for communicating in French:
Répétez, s'il vous plaît. = Repeat, please.
Une minute, s'il vous plaît.= Wait a minute, please.
Comment dit-on [English word] en français? = How do you say [English word] in French.?
Que veut dire [French word]?= What does [French word] mean ?
Qu'est-ce que c'est? = What's that?
Je ne sais pas. = I don't know.
Je ne comprends pas. = I don't understand.

HOMEWORK: Each module of the textbook is divided into two parts: 1) White Pages (Thèmes et pratiques de conversation, Structures utiles, Pespectives culturelles, Lecture, Un pas en avant) and 2) Green Pages (Structures)

Before each class, read through the white pages so you know what we will be doing in class. There are also reading exercises in the white pages to write out and turn in which are listed on the syllabus. I will correct these. (If you are having trouble with the reading questions, try writing out the question and translating it into English. I'll correct that, too.) You also need to write out the answers to exercises in the green pages. Check this work using Appendix B (page 453 in the textbook). All exercises should be corrected clearly in a different color. If there are no errors, just put a check mark so I know that you've corrected your work. You will turn in the corrected work from the green pages daily and all other written assignments when indicated on the syllabus. Full credit will be given only for corrected work turned in on time.

On your own, you will also do the written exercises assigned in the Workbook and the Activités de compréhension et de prononication in the Lab Manual corresponding to each Module. Suggested completion dates are indicated on the syllabus. You may choose to do the even or odd questions for each written exercise in the Workbook section, unless it is a paragraph or dialogue or you find you need more review. Correct your Workbook/Lab Manual exercises using the Answer Key. All exercises should be corrected clearly in a different color. Mark any sections you don't understand so that you can ask about them in class or I can explain when I'm correcting your work. Bring your Workbook/Lab Manual to each exam so that I can give you credit for your work. You will get full credit (10 points) for complete and corrected (in a different color) exercises and partial credit (5 points) for uncorrected exercises or late lab work.

Exercises in the Lab Manual are to be completed with the audio available in the Language Lab and online. See the section Language Lab below.

HOW TO DO WELL IN CLASS:

1) Prepare assignments before coming to class so we can get the most out of our class time together. Assignments are to be completed as indicated on the syllabus. It is nearly impossible to “cram” for this course and do well. Learning a language is like building a house of cards: each one must be well in place before adding the next. I urge you to spend at least 30 minutes on French per day, rather than studying all at once before exams. Regular repetition of small quantities of information is the most successful method for learning a foreign language.

2) Participate actively in each lesson. Mentally answer each and every question when a classmate is responding. Class participation and homework make up 25% of your grade. The more you participate, the better your grade will be, and the better your French will become. Your class grade reflects your performance in class (attendance, knowledge of daily lesson, volunteering answers, asking questions, class reflections). Take listening comprehension seriously — it will help both your proficiency and your grade (there is a listening component on exams).

3) Take risks. Speak out. Don’t be afraid to make mistakes. I am here to help. The more you speak, the more you will get out of the class. Aim for perfection when writing. The desire to communicate is the most important consideration when speaking.

4) Speak French as much as possible in the classroom. Speak French outside the classroom whenever possible, too. Listen to French music, go to French movies, eat French food!

5) Bring textbook to class every day. This is essential. You should also purchase a decent quality French/English-English/French dictionary. While not required, it will help you find words not explained in the textbook.

6) Take notes in class whenever appropriate. Buy 3x5 notecards to make personalized verb charts and other study aids. Study together in pairs groups outside of class.
