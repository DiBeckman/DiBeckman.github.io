Il faut cultiver notre jardin.
It is necessary to cultivate our garden.
We must cultivate our garden.

“If we must cultivate our garden, it is because all is not lost, we can still hope for a harvest.”
André Morize “The ‘Moment’ of Candide”

“We must cultivate our garden is Candide’s lesson, not Candide’s.”
Theodore E. D. Braun “Interpreting Candide: The Anvil of Controversy”

“Eldorado, surrounded by impossible mountains, is as if it never existed, a Utopian country peopled by Utopian men. For man as he really is, there is only one garden, one paradise: Candide’s.”
René Pomeau "Providence, Pessimism, and Absurdity”

“The final establishment of a small, self-sufficient, hard-working community is the result of decisions made by each individual to contribute in the most appropriate way to the well-being of the group… The closing scene emphasizes that, in Voltaire’s eyes, each of us is ultimately responsible for the well-being of humankind.”
Jean Perkins “Intellectual Ideas Raised in Candide”

"The negative aspect of Candide is nowhere more evident than in the last sentence which seems to convey a message, but so cryptic that no two scholars have agreed on its meaning, for the very simple reason that the author, having destroyed a system, was not ready to offer a straight answer to unsolvable problems. We should note that his silence did not affect the quality of the work or the efficiency of its propaganda.”
Jean Sareil “The Comic Writing in Candide"

--

From Luke Carter, Spring 2011
