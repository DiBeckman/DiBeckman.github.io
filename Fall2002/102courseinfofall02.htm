WELCOME TO FRENCH! This course aims at giving you a solid foundation in understanding, speaking, reading and writing the French language at the elementary level. To accomplish these goals, it is essential that we use French as the language of the classroom. Handy phrases for communicating in class:

Répétez, s'il vous plaît. = Repeat, please.
Comment dit-on [English word]? = How do you say [English word] in French?
Que veut dire [French word]?= What does [French word] mean ?
Qu'est-ce que c'est? = What's that?
Je ne sais pas. = I don't know.
Je ne comprends pas. = I don't understand.

HOMEWORK: Each chapter of the textbook is divided into two parts: 1) Activités et lectures followed by the Vocabulaire section (white pages) and 2) Grammaire et exercices pages (blue pages).

Before each class, you need to prepare the following:

1) Read and write out the exercises in the appropriate sections of the blue pages. You may choose to do either even or odd questions in each exercise. Check your work using a pen or pencil in a different color using the Answer Key in Appendix D of the text book (p. 449). I will correct the "Answers may vary" exercises. In the margin, jot down any questions you have as you correct your work. If you are having problems with a particular section, do all the questions.

2) Do the written and oral exercises assigned in the Cahier. Exercises with a cassette symbol are to be completed with the tapes available in the Foreign Language Technology Center. It's much better to listen to the tapes in short sessions several times during the week than do everything in one long session. If you don't understand the directions for an exercise, please check with me. You may do even or odd questions in each exercise. With the listening exercises, it may be easier to do them all, rather than to try to do even and odd.

Correct your workbook exercises using the Answer Key purchased with your textbook. All exercises should be corrected clearly in a different color. I will correct the "Answers may vary" and writing exercises. Mark any sections you don't understand so that you can ask about them in class or I can explain when I'm correcting your work

Due dates for homework (in both textbook and Cahier) are listed on the syllabus. Full credit will be given only for corrected work turned in on time.

We will cover chapters 6-10 in FLF 102.

HOW TO DO WELL IN CLASS:

1. Prepare all your assignments before coming to class so that we can devote our in-class sessions to communicative activities rather than learning grammar. Assignments are due for class on the day indicated on the syllabus. Don't get behind or allow yourself to stay confused. It's much easier to keep up than to catch up!

2. ACTIVELY participate in each lesson. Mentally answer each and every question by practicing each answer under your breath while a classmate is responding.

3. TAKE RISKS!! Speak out. Don't be afraid to make mistakes. The more you speak, the better you will get. The more fun you have speaking French, the more you will learn.

4. Speak French in the classroom. Speak French outside of the classroom at every opportunity.

5. Do not chew gum in class. Having something in your mouth makes it harder to use correct pronunciation.

6. TAKE NOTES. Write down everything from the blackboard. If you don't have enough time in class to get everything down, I will be happy to share materials with you after class or in my office.

I look forward to a pleasant and productive semester! Don't hesitate to ask me for help and assistance in person or by email.
