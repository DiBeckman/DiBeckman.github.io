Expressionism
In Germany, between 1910 and 1925. Precursors in painting: Van Gogh, Gauguin and Munch; in literature: Baudelaire, Rimbaud, Dostoevsky, and Nietzsche.

Radical revolt against subject matter and style of Realism. Artist presents a troubled or intensely emotional vision of human life and human society by violently exaggerating and distorting objective features of the outer world and embodying extremes of mood and feeling. Man often stands alone and afraid in an industrial, technological, and urban society which is disintegrating into chaos. In fiction, standard modes of characterization and plot are abandoned for symbolic figures involved in a dream-world of nightmarish events.
