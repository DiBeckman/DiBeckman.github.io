Art Tour of Paris presentation:
There are two parts to this assignment, one that you will present during the second class session and the other that will begin now and continue through our trip to Paris.

Part One
The first part of this assignment is to pick one piece of art from each of the four museums that we will visit (Cluny, Louvre, D'Orsay, and Pompidou.  There are two tools you can use to identify your four artworks:

• Our text books, Rick Steve's Paris 2010 and Art for Travellers: France
• Visit the museum websites (see the links to these sites below)

Once you have identified your artworks, research each piece of art and its artist as well as the historical/artistic significance of each artwork . Record your findings in your Class Portfolio.  Please include your reasoning and rationale for your choices in your findings as well.  We want you to begin to develop a relationship with the four works that you choose.  This relationship will begin now and will culminate with visiting each of your selected pieces in their respective museums while we are in Paris.  We expect that you will have at least two (if not more) entries in your portfolio about your works of art; one recording your initial research and rationale and a second reflecting on your visit to the museum.

Part Two

From the four artworks that you chose for Part One of this assignment, we would like you to choose one and prepare a two minute talk that you will give during our second class meeting.  We will be strict about this time limit. Your presentation should answer the following questions (only):

• What is the title of the artwork?
• Who is the artist?
• Where is the artwork located?
• Why did you choose this artwork?

Once you have identified the artwork that you want to present to the class, email Dr. Beckman to let her know the artwork you have chosen -- you will sign-up on a first-come, first-serve basis. Deadline: midnight on Friday, Feb. 19. Once your choice has been confirmed by Dr. Beckman, find a picture of your piece of art online and email the link to Matthew.  We will project your artwork while you are giving your two minute talk - no need to worry about making your own PowerPoint or printing out a copy of the artwork. 

If you have any questions, please let Dr. G, Dr. B, or Matthew know.
