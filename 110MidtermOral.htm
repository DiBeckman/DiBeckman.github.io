FLF 110:     MIDTERM ORAL EXAM

The midterm oral exam is designed to make you feel good about your ability to communicate in French using the material that you have studied so far during the semester. While you prepare, think about how much you have learned and what you can say in French, and feel confident about your abilities! Remember that individuals with growth mindsets are willing to take learning risks and understand that through practice and effort-sometimes a lot of effort-their abilities can improve. Those with growth mindsets believe that their brains are malleable, that intelligence and abilities constantly grow, and that only time will tell how smart they will become.

There will be three parts to the exam: a skit with a partner(s), presenting a portrait and answering questions individually. Your grade will be determined using the Oral Exam Rubric.

• Skit with a partner:

With your partner(s), you will choose a situation and write a skit in French to perform. The skit should be no more than 5 minutes long. You may use props, but you should not read whole sentences from your notes. You will perform the skit twice.

For the skit, choose one of the following Situations from our textbook:

Situation 1 or 2, page 81
Situations 1, 2, 4 or 5 page 143
Au restaurant, page 212 (with or without the waiter) See more restaurant vocabulary if you like.

• Presenting a portrait:

You will choose a portrait from the North Carolina Museum of Art to describe. A portrait is defined as "a painting, photograph, sculpture, or other artistic representation of a person, in which the face and its expression is predominant. The intent is to display the likeness, personality, and even the mood of the person," so please review how to describe people physically and psychologically (Modules 1, 3). This page looks at how portrait paintings have changed over time.l See the North Carolina Museum of Art-FLF 110 page for details about the museum and the assignment. Bring a copy of your portrait to the exam. (You can download it from the museum web site, or take one yourself at the museum: "Still photography of the permanent collection, taken in existing light, is permitted on condition that the photographs are for personal, noncommercial use.") Do not choose the same portrait as your partner(s).
