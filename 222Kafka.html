Kafka: a few quotes and links

"Any purely ideological reading of Kafka… will miss [his] most original contribution: the figuration of that which dooms interpretation to failure, even a correct one… Gregor’s abjection is…more than a symptom to be read and decoded, more than a condensation of social forces or contradictions, and thus more than any scapegoat figure. He remains a foreign object in the text and in any interpretation." Eric Santer

“That is finally what it means to be alone. To spin oneself into the silk of one’s own soul, to mask oneself in one’s own cocoon, and wait for the metamorphosis, which never fails to come about.” August Strindberg

Instead of the “strange beautiful butterfly of Strindberg’s fin de siècle imagination,” we have “a grotesque, primitive ornament of modernity: the monstrous vermin.” Mark Anderson

Possible interpretations of The Metamorphosis (from different classes over the years)

Gregor slides down the evolutionary ladder; there is a parody/ paradox of rationality as the biological, entomological takes over.

Psychological fable: a son rebelling against his father; the self is determined socially.

Marxist: alienation from capitalist culture; a father sells his children; Gregor is so conditioned to his identity of being bought and sold that he accepts his fate passively; condemnation of socialism: Gregor is like a government that provides for capable, but lazy parasites …

Feminist: What happens when women take the power formerly held by men?

Religious: Gregor is Jesus sacrificing himself for his family’s sake.

Cultural/historical: impending WWII holocaust against the outsider, the Jew, the handicapped

Formalist: hour glass structure: Meg takes Gregor’s place in the family; optimistic or pessimistic ending

Reader response: What does the reader HOPE will happen? Will the Samsa family ever return to normal?

Queer: How does the way Gregor is treated resemble a the coming out of gay child in a homophobic family?
