Updated December 31, 2012

  • Read across the chart to see the assignments due that day. Module and page numbers in columns 2, 3, and 4 refer to the textbook; page numbers in the final column refer to the Workbook/Lab Manual.
  • White and green pages in the textbook (columns 2 and 4) should be completed before coming to class. Written work from the green pages should be turned in at the beginning of class.
  • Compositions, one per module, are also listed in the second column and should be turned in on the date indicated. All compositions will be revised by you based on my corrections, and turned in again within the next week. I will give you a numerical grade on your composition beginning with Module 3.
  • The third column indicates reading exercises in the white pages to turn in with other daily written homework.
  • The fourth column lists the written exercises in the green pages to be completed and corrected in a different color using Appendix B, p. 491 ff. in the textbook. Turn these exercises in daily as indicated on the syllabus. Errors in the current edition of the textbook and answer key can be found here.
  • The final column lists pages in the Workbook/Lab Manual. The Activités Écrites (pp. 1-108) are listed first and Actvités de compréhension et de prononication (pp. 149-212) are listed second. The audio for the workbook is delivered via the Cengage website http://login.cengagebrain.com. It is also available on our Moodle site: http://moodle.wolfware.ncsu.edu/ Correct your work using the Workbook Answer Key which is posted on our Moodle site and will be on reserve at DH Hill. YOU are responsible for keeping up with the workbook exercises corresponding to the material covered in class. Bring your completed and corrected work to class on exam days to receive full credit.
  • You are also responsible for keeping up with our second textbook English Grammar for Students of French (EGSF). Assigned chapters are listed below on the syllabus in conjunction with each exam. Use the French_review_Booklet.pdf to find both the exercises and answers. Some chapters do not have associated exercises. Bring your answers (in the book or on paper) to class on exam days, too, in order to receive full credit for completed, corrected exercises.
