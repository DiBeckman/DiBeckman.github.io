FLF 110 Syllabus; Fall 2010
Updated August 25, 2010

• Read across the chart to see the assignments due that day. Module and page numbers in columns 2 through 4 refer to the first part of the textbook.
• White and green pages in the textbook (columns 2 and 4) should be completed before coming to class. Written work from the green pages should be turned in at the beginning of class.
• Compositions, one per module, are also listed in the second column and should be turned in on the date indicated. All compositions will be revised by you based on my corrections, and turned in again within the next week. I will give you a numerical grade on your composition beginning with Module 3.
• The third column indicates reading exercises in the white pages to turn in with other daily written homework.
• The fourth column lists the written exercises in the green pages to be completed and corrected in a different color using Appendix B, p. 491 ff. in the textbook. Turn these exercises in daily as indicated on the syllabus.
• The final column lists pages in the Workbook/Lab Manual which is located in the back of our custom edition textbook. The Activités Ecrites (pp. 1-108) are listed first and Actvités de compréhension et de prononication (pp. 149-220) are listed second. The audio for the workbook is delivered via the Cengage website http://login.cengagebrain.com. It is also available on our Moodle site: http://moodle.wolfware.ncsu.edu/. Correct your work using the Workbook Answer key which is on reserve at DH Hill and also posted on our Moodle site. YOU are responsible for keeping up with the workbook exercises corresponding to the material covered in class. Bring your completed, corrected lab work to class on exam days to receive full credit. If you don't want to write in your textbook, you may wish to do these exercises on a separate sheet of paper.
• You are also responsible for keeping up with our second textbook English Grammar for Students of French (EGSF). Assigned chapters are listed below on the syllabus in conjunction with each exam. If you have the 5th edition, exercises are at the end of the chapter and corrections are in the back of the book. If you have the 6th edition, use the French_review_Booklet.pdf for both the exercises and answers. Bring your answers (in the book or on paper) to class on exam days, too, in order to receive credit for completed, corrected exercises.
