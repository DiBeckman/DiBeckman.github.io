FLF 110 ORAL PART OF FINAL EXAM

The oral part of the final exam will be held during the last week of class. You and a partner (or two) will sign up for a time slot. We will not be having regularly scheduled classes on those days.

Instructions: prepare all three of the following topics of conversation and be ready to have a conversation with your partner(s). You and your partner will draw one topic for the exam. You will have five minutes to prepare, once the topic is chosen.

Themes of conversation and suggestions are listed below for each topic. Feel free to make your skit as original and as personal as possible. You may bring props if you wish. Both students should ask questions as well as make statements. You should ask 4-5 questions each. Ask complete questions, not just "et toi ?"' You may refer to brief notes during your skit, but you should not read whole sentences from your notes.

Some useful vocabulary to look at: invitations: 131; restaurant: 134-135 & 209-10; telling and reacting to a story: 163; traditional holidays: 232; travel agency: 276; hotel: 334-335.

You will also present an art work by a French artist, and you will answer some of the questions from the list below the list of skits.

• Skit with a partner:

I. Au restaurant
Waiter in a French restaurant / Client(s)

Goal: Use present tense + food vocabulary (with appropriate articles: le/la/les, du/de la/de l'/des, etc.) The waiter and client use vous with each other. The friends can use tu or vous when they are talking together.

The waiter:
*asks if the client is ready / wants to order
*asks for client's choice of appetizer, main course, dessert, and drink(s)
*recommends the daily special
*is very polite and asks about quality of food
*asks if client wants more of the food and drinks, responds to client's problems
*brings check
*specifies if service is included
*asks about mode of payment chosen by the client
*thanks

The client:
*asks for menu
*asks about daily special or recommended daily special
*orders appetizer, main course, dessert, and drink(s)
*comments on the quality of the food / beverages
* finds a problem (a mistake, doesn't like food) and complains
*asks for check
*asks if service is included
*asks about acceptable forms of payment
*pays and leaves or does not leave a tip (says why)
*leaves and says good-bye

II. A l'agence de voyage

Make arrangements for a trip to a part of France or to another Francophone country.

Goal: Both the travel agent and the traveler(s) will make at least three recommendations using il faut, il est nécessaire de, il vaut mieux etc... Each person will also use three verbs in the future proche or future (Module 11) tense. The travel agent and the client use vous with each other, as in any business situation. The friends can use tu or vous when they are talking together.

The travel agent:
*asks about destination and dates of departure (class of ticket)
*proposes a flight ticket with simple itinerary; says when ticket will be ready
*answers questions about passport, visa, etc.
*asks if customer needs to reserve a hotel or a car, how the customer will go sightseeing in the country of destination
*proposes hotels with different amenities
*suggests special items to buy as souvenirs
*recommends places to see, things to do and gives general safety recommendations
*wishes a pleasant flight and thanks

The traveler:
*inquires about and reserves a round-trip plane ticket to the chosen destination
*finds out the price and schedule
*asks if passport, travelers checks, and credit cards are needed
*asks travel agent to reserve a hotel, specifies type of hotel, room, and amenities desired
*asks information about the hotel and about sights, or things to do, asks for brochures
*discusses special items to buy as souvenirs
*may ask about renting a car or about other ways of sightseeing
*thanks and leaves

III. Quand j'étais jeune . . .

Two (three) friends discuss their childhood and their youth.

Goal: Use imparfait and passé composé to talk about the past. Students should talk about what they used to do (imparfait) as well as about a specific incident that happened one day (passé composé) . Use tu since you are talking with a friend.

Discuss:
*where you used to live
*what you used to do everyday
*what you used to do to have fun
*what you and your family used to do on vacation
*your family's traditions for special holidays
*when you were in high school, your activities at school and after school
*your relationship to your sisters/brothers, friends, teachers
*relate one specific incident that happened one day (passé composé)

• Art work presentation:
Each student will choose and present a work created by a French artist in the collection of the North Carolina Museum of Art. You may choose a different work than those listed on the NCMA-110 page, as long as the work is by a French artist. Do some research on the artist and story portrayed, if there is one. Bring a copy of the work with you to the exam. Do not choose the same work as your partner(s).
