Film and Video Information

FILM: Everyone will be required to see Cyrano de Bergerac, a French-language film with subtitles.
It will be shown the week of April 1-5, Monday, Tuesday & Thursday at 4 PM and 7 PM and on Wednesday & Friday at 3:30 PM only in the Media Center on the 2nd floor of Erdahl-Cloyd Wing of the NC State Library. On Tuesday April 9, turn in a one-page, typed commentary (in French) of the film. You may use the questions in Section 4 on the film study sheet.

VIDEOS: You will be required to choose at least four cultural videos to view during the semester. All videos are on reserve at the Media Center on the second floor of the Erdahl-Cloyd Wing of the NC State Library. Each week, one video (or set of two videos) will be shown at 4 PM on Monday and Tuesday. You may go to see the video(s) at that time. In an emergency, if you are unable to see the video(s) at the scheduled time, you may be able to arrange for a personal viewing at another time.

After you have seen the video(s), you must submit a one-page, typed commentary in French. (That is, one page for each video or set of 2 videos. In the case of the latter, your commentary will be 1/2 page long for each video.) Please do not summarize the video. You may discuss what intrigued or surprised you about the cultural content, what you thought about the content from an American perspective, how the video content fits with your conception of the French culture, etc. Please note: You may choose any four (or more) videos (or sets of videos) according to your interest. You may hand in your typed commentaries any time following the showing of the video. However, you must hand in at least two videos by TH Feb. 21. The last two (or more) videos must be handed in by TH April 18. See the schedule below for dates and titles of videos.

VIDEOS FOR FLF 201: SPRING 2001

January 23-24: [Shown Wednesday and Thursday, this week only.] Chanel, Chanel: (English, 60 minutes) The world of fashion has produced no more charismatic figure than Coco Chanel, the famous fashion designer whose sense of style, and simplicity and ease of clothing became her hallmarks. Using rare archival footage, this program explores the course of her career as well as the fascinating story of her personal life.

January 28-29: A Day in the Country: Impressionism and the French Landscape (English, 30 minutes) An overview of the Impressionist movement in painting: the beginnings, the leading artists, the masterpieces. and Monet: Legacy of Light (English, 27 minutes) Focuses on the relationship of Monet, the artist with his surroundings including actual footage of Monet painting.

February 4-5: Van Gogh Biography (English, 45 minutes) The story of the mad genius of painting including his famed run-in with his ear! Though he is now worldfamous, he sold only one canvas in his lifetime. Hear Van Gogh's own words - taken from his letters to his brother - as he explains his life and work. and Berthe Morisot: The Forgotten Impressionist (English, 32 minutes) Although she was at the center of the Parisian avant-garde painting and influenced Renoir, Manet, and Degas, when she died, her death certificate listed her as having "no profession". An interesting biography of a painter whose talent was often overshadowed by her womanhood. (Read p. 61 and p. 64 before seeing the video.)

February 11-12: Camille Claudel (French with English subtitles, 159 minutes - feature film) A historically accurate depiction of two great sculptors of modern art: Camille Claudel and Auguste Rodin. Both an inspiring saga of artistic vision and the haunting story of a doomed romance, it offers a brilliant study of the thin line separating creativity, passion, and madness.(Several scenes of nude models.) (Read p. 65 before viewing the video.)

February 18-19: The Biography of Picasso (English, 90 minutes) A fascinating look at the life and work of the most influential artist of the 20th century. Intriguing parallels between his life and his art.

February 25-26: The Mystery of Picasso (Some French, mostly music, 77 minutes) Experience the mysterious connection between the artist's vision and its realization on canvas as Picasso creates over 15 works before your very eyes. These unique creations were destroyed after the filming!

March 18-19: Daily Life in the Court of Versailles (English, 57 minutes) Learn the story of the famous kings of France (Louis XIV, XV, XVI) and their opulent life at Versailles. (Read pp. 140-141 before seeing the video.)
