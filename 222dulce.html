dulce et utile | sweet and useful

the ultimate goal of poetry:
to please and to instruct; to seduce and to educate; to entertain and to teach

omne tulit punctum qui miscuit utile dulci
lectorem delectando pariterque monendo (343-4)

But the writer who has combined the pleasant with the useful wins on all points by delighting the reader while he gives advice

…aut prodesse volunt aut delectare poetae
aut simul et iucunda et idonea dicere vitae (333-4)

Poets aim either to help or to amuse the reader, or to say what is pleasant and at the same time what is suitable.

Horace (c.13 B.C. ) Ars Poetica, Epistula ad Pisones
