Madame Bovary: a collection of terms and questions to ponder

Bovarysme: title of book by Jules de Gautier (1902). It is the habit of conceiving ourselves other than we are; the practice of imagining life as more perfect than it can be and then being bored or angry and rejecting it; characters who attempt to take the desires of other people (or other characters) as their own, seeing them as models to imitate.

Counterpoint: The technique of combining two or more melodic lines in such a way that they establish a harmonic relationship while retaining their linear individuality.

Erich Auerbach The Realism of Flaubert

The very day after the book appeared, people demanded to know the identity of Madame Bovary. When Flaubert feeling persecuted, answered with a statement that was in effect the simple truth—“I am Madame Bovary!” everyone assumed he was joking. Yet could anything be more true, more certain?

“My poor Bovary, without a doubt, is suffering and weeping at this very instant in twenty villages of France.”

To what extent are Emma’s problems those of women of her time, place, and social class?

“[The novel's] object is to make Emma’s existence as intelligible and visible as it may be. We who read the book are to share her sense of life, till no uncertainty is left in it; we are to see and understand her experience, and to see her while she enjoys and endures it; we are to be placed within her world, to get the immediate taste of it, and outside her world as well, to get the full effect, more of it than she herself could see.” Percy Lubbock, The Craft of Fiction in Madame Bovary (p. 353).

How would you defend Flaubert against the charges of immorality for which he was charged? Is there a didactic purpose in his book? Does Flaubert treat Emma’s death as damnation for her sins? her dreams? Is Emma a tragic figure?

Rousseau et Flaubert
Rousseau: Je sens mon cœur et je connais les hommes. (I feel my own heart and I know how men are.)
Flaubert: Notre cœur ne doit être bon qu’à sentir celui des autres. (Our heart is good only for feeling the hearts of others.)
