• Read across the chart to see the assignments due each day. Module and page numbers in the first three columns refer to the textbook.
• White and green pages in the textbook (columns 1 and 3) should be completed before coming to class. Written work from the green pages should be turned in at the beginning of class.
• Compositions, one for each module, are also listed in the first column and should be turned in on the date indicated. All compositions will be revised by you based on my corrections, and turned in again within the next week. I will give a grade for compositions beginning with Module 3.
• The second column indicates reading exercises in the white pages to turn in with other written homework.
• The third column lists the written exercises in the green pages to be completed and corrected in a different color using Appendix B, p. 485 ff. in the textbook. Turn these exercises in daily as indicated on the syllabus.
• The fourth column lists pages in the Workbook/Lab Manual. The Activités Ecrites (pp. 1-134) are listed first and Actvités de compréhension et de prononication (pp. 135-233) are listed second. Correct this work using the Workbook Answer key pdf and the LabManual Answer Key.pdf YOU are responsible for keeping up with the workbook exercises corresponding to the material covered in class. Bring your workbook to class on exam days.
• You are also responsible for keeping up with our second textbook English Grammar for Students of French (EGSF). The Motifs Correlation pdf will tell you which pages to read and exercises to do for each Module. Bring this book to class on exam days, too, so that I can give you credit for completed, corrected exercises.
