For this project, you will use the Internet to research a French-speaking country, prepare a handout for your classmates, complete a written assignment (Composition 9), and orally present in French to the class on the due date. Please see the Peer Evaluation Form for the class presentation. The writing assignment will be graded according to the Writing Assessment Rubric. You will also be responsible for answering questions about a pays francophone (not the one you presented) on Exam 4.

1. Decide whether to do the project on your own or with a partner.

2. Choose one of the French-speaking countries (les pays francophones) from the list below. Sign up on Google Docs. First come, first served. See the maps at the beginning of our textbook to locate your country.

3. See the assignment Expression écrite on page 279 of the textbook, and follow the 5 steps listed as preparation for your handout and composition. (See #5 below.)

Once you've learned basic information about your country, using French web sites will actually make it easier to create your handout, since you won't make translation mistakes. If you can't find the answer to a particular question, find another interesting or relevant fact. See Module 4 to review leisure activities, Module 5 to review weather, and Module 9 for travel vocabulary. You must also include two current events relevant to your country. For example, you could include one from the past year and one from the past week. Current events can be presented in English, since the vocabulary is likely to be unfamiliar.

4. Prepare a one-page handout for your classmates with highlights or interesting facts about your country. See the questions that will be on Exam 4 below. The handout should be at least 50% in French. You will turn in a draft of the handout the week before the presentation, so that I can proofread the French. You must also include your sources on the handout, so as not to violate Academic Integrity.

5. Composition 9 to be turned in the day of your presentation. On a separate sheet, each student writes two paragraphs to tell about your country (Part I) and the (imaginary) trip you took there (Part II). Skip lines or double space. Use present tense verbs to describe the country (Part I) and past tense verbs (passé composé and imparfait) to say what you did there (Part II). If you are working with a partner, you can collaborate, but each student should turn in his/her own composition.

6. On the due date, you (and your partner) will orally present your country to the class. Be prepared to point out your country on the world map provided. Come in costume, bring a food, music, print out pictures and maps or make a poster to illustrate your presentation. Speak directly to the class using notes and your handout and visual aids, rather than reading your report! The other students will be filling out the Peer Evaluation Form looking for a quality presentation. Each student will also turn in Composition 9 the day of his/her presentation.

Composition 9: The written portion of the assignment. Here are some questions to help you organize your composition, but you do not need to answer each one. You do, however, need an introductory sentence and a conclusion. Above, all write about what makes your country unique and interesting.

I. Décrivez le pays (au présent)
Comment s’appellent le pays et ses habitants? Où se trouve le pays?
Décrivez la géographie du pays (Il y a des montagnes au nord, un désert au sud, etc.)
Décrivez le climat en été. Quel temps fait-il? Quelles activités pouvez-vous faire en éte? Quels sports ou passe-temps…?
Décrivez le climat en hiver. Quel temps fait-il? Quelles activités pouvez-vous faire en hiver? Quels sports ou passe-temps…?
Décrivez l'économie du pays (riche? pauvre?). Quels sont ses produits principaux?
Quelles langues parle-t-on dans ce pays?
Quelle est la religion principale?

II. Décrivez votre voyage. (au passé )
Qu'est-ce que vous avez fait quand vous étiez à votre destination?
Quelles villes avez-vous visité? Quel(s) cadeau(x) avez-vous acheté(s)? Pour qui?
Quels sites touristiques avez-vous visité? (naturels ou culturels)
Qu'est-ce que vous avez mangé? Quelles sont les spécialités régionales?
