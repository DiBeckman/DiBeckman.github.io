Rubric for entries about reading & films
Please include the following information for each entry:

1. Just the Facts
Title:
Author/ Director:
Date:
Main Characters: with a one sentence description of each.

2. Summary: one-three paragraphs.
For fiction: What happens? For nonfiction: What is the main argument of the work?

3. Appreciation and Insights
How would you rate this work? What comments do you have about the style, content, uniqueness of this work?

Cultural or historical insights (examples):
• What did you learn about Paris? What did you learn about France?
• What did you learn about the historical period?
• Social classes?
• Daily life?
• Cultural Values?
• Does this work lead you to think about American history or culture in a new way?

Questions to consider for films:

How does the title function? Is the English title simply a translation of the French?

Determine the dominant kinds of shots in a film (close-up, medium shot, long shot, etc.) and consider how these shots affect your responses to the characters and/or action depicted?

Does the camera move or remain stationary? How do camera movements or lack of them affect your responses?

How does the visual information (ie. details of the frame) of an image determine meaning?

How long are the shots in a particular film, and how do long takes or rapid montages affect our responses?

How does the editing (way in which shots are joined together) affect your emotional and/or intellectual responses?

How does the music affect your emotional response in particular sequences?

Consider the effectiveness of the actors in films. For instance, some actors are brilliant in theater but not always so in film. Why?

Consider the relationships of sound to the images.

Do the costumes just look “nice,” or do they help support characterization and/or theme?
